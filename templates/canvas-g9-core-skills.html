UNIT 5 — SCIENCE OF MASTERY

The "Grade 9 Trifecta" is trust, focus and practice. Without these three mindset approaches or if any of them are missing, then the process weakens and that reduces our chances of scoring the top grades.
"Trust the process" means that we take care of every single step of the process, always doing our best to score 100% and give the best effort that we possibly can, not worrying about getting to the end but focusing more on every single step leading towards the top grades. This is because ultimately there is no end and the result is actually the product of a strong process that's been followed diligently. A strong result doesn't arrive by itself; it is the product of a good process followed well.
One of the three pillars of success is feedback and what's important about feedback is that we're not always only relying on other people to give us feedback but that we are actively participating in the feedback process and learning ourselves how that actually works so that we can get better at the process of self-improving and self-correcting.
Anders Ericsson said, "The right sort of practice carried out always sufficient period of time leads to improvement nothing else." This points to the idea that we cannot simply rely on being passive participants in the learning process but that we need to be diligently active in it and actually get better at the skill of practice itself.
Re-reading and highlighting feel productive because we're doing something when we're doing those but in reality, unless we are actively retrieving information from our memories as much as possible, highlighting and rereading of very passive activities. There is a time and a place for them; for example, if you are engaging in exam practice and you are highlighting key information in a text or then that's important but leading up to the exams you really need to be focusing on developing the skills, such as practicing writing full essays with introductions, body paragraphs, conclusions, creative writing and so on and being extremely diligent about every single element in those. In that sort of case highlighting and rereading are not going to be very helpful or at least not as helpful as actually practicing the core skills themselves.
Retrieval is a powerful science-backed learning technique which requires you to recall information from memory or pull it out from memory rather than simply re-reading. The idea is that the more fluently we can retrieve information from memory and the more we can do it over time, the more the information sticks in our memory for longer.
Spaced repetition is the idea or the concept that we need to keep coming back to topics over time and practicing recalling it. Doubling this with retrieval practice helps with developing a stronger memory.
The Feynman technique suggests that if we can explain a concept in simple terms such as explaining it to a five-year-old then we probably understand the concept very well.
