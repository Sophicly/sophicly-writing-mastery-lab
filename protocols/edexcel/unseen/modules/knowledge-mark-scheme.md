## **2. Edexcel Mark Scheme Descriptors**

### **2.A. Q12 Mark Scheme (20 marks — AO1: 8 marks, AO2: 12 marks)**

**Question format:** "Compare the ways the writers present [theme] in Poem 1: [Title] and Poem 2: [Title]. In your answer, you should compare: the ideas in the poems; the poets' use of language; the poets' use of form and structure." [20]

| Level | Mark Range | Comparison | AO1 (8 marks) — Personal Response & Critical Style | AO2 (12 marks) — Language, Form & Structure |
|-------|-----------|-----------|--------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------|
| Level 5 | 17-20 | Perceptive comparisons and contrasts, varied and comprehensive range of similarities and differences. | Assured personal response, high level of engagement, discerning choice of references. Critical style developed with maturity, perceptive understanding and interpretation. | Perceptive grasp of form and structure and their effect. Cohesive evaluation of poets' language and effects. Subject terminology integrated and precise. |
| Level 4 | 13-16 | Effective comparisons and contrasts, wide range of similarities and differences supported throughout. | Developed personal response, thorough engagement, well-chosen references. Critical style sustained, well-developed interpretation. | Analysis of form and structure and their effect is sustained. Comments effectively on poets' use of language and effect on reader. Terminology accurate and appropriate. |
| Level 3 | 9-12 | Range of comparisons and contrasts, some similarities and differences considered. | Relevant personal response, soundly related to text, focused references. Appropriate critical style, sound interpretation. | Sound understanding of form and structure linked to effect. Clear awareness of how poets use language and effect on reader. Relevant terminology used. |
| Level 2 | 5-8 | Some underdeveloped comparisons, obvious similarities and differences. | Some elements of personal response, some reference without consistent focus. Some evidence of critical style. | Some comment on form and structure. Some awareness of poets' use of language without development. Limited terminology. |
| Level 1 | 1-4 | Little or no comparison. | Simple with little personal response, little relevant reference. Little evidence of critical style. | Minimal identification of form and structure. Little awareness of language. Little evidence of terminology. |
| 0 | 0 | Nothing worthy of credit. | — | — |

**[AI_INTERNAL] CRITICAL NOTES:**
- AO2 carries 12 of 20 marks (60%) — analysis of methods is weighted MORE heavily than personal response
- Responses that are considerably unbalanced (mostly about one poem) cannot access Level 3+
- The mark scheme explicitly assesses FORM AND STRUCTURE as separate from LANGUAGE — both must be addressed
- Comparison is not a discrete AO but is assessed through how well AO1 and AO2 are demonstrated comparatively

## **2.C. Q12 Comparative Essay Structure & Criteria**

### **Q12 Essay Structure: 5 Paragraphs (Comparative)**

The student writes a comparative essay in response to Q12:

1. **Introduction** — Comparative thesis statement only (1-2 sentences maximum)
2. **Comparative Body Paragraph 1** — FORM comparison using comparative TTECEA
3. **Comparative Body Paragraph 2** — LANGUAGE comparison using comparative TTECEA
4. **Comparative Body Paragraph 3** — STRUCTURE comparison using comparative TTECEA
5. **Conclusion** — Restated comparative thesis only (1-2 sentences maximum)

**[AI_INTERNAL] CRITICAL STRUCTURAL NOTES:**

- **Form is MANDATORY** — Edexcel explicitly requires "the poets' use of form and structure." Unlike AQA/EDUQAS unseen poetry, form is NOT optional. Every student must write about form.
- **Body paragraph focus matches the question bullets:** Ideas (covered through conceptual topic sentences), Language (Body 2), Form and Structure (Bodies 1 and 3)
- **AO2 dominance:** AO2 carries 12/20 marks (60%). The protocol must emphasise close analysis of methods.
- **Minimal intro/conclusion:** At 20 marks and 45 minutes, students cannot afford elaborate introductions or conclusions. The thesis IS the introduction; the restated thesis IS the conclusion.
- **ALL paragraphs are comparative** — this is a single comparative question, not a single-poem-plus-comparison structure.

### **Introduction Criteria (1 mark)**

* **Comparative Thesis Statement**: A clear, precise thesis comparing BOTH poems' approaches to the shared theme, signposting the three comparative body paragraphs (Form, Language, Structure). Must reference both poets/poems and establish the key comparative argument. 1-2 sentences maximum. (AO1)
* **No separate hook or theme sentence** — at 20 marks, every sentence must count. The thesis IS the introduction.

### **Comparative Body Paragraph Criteria (6 marks each)**

* **Comparative Topic Sentence**: Opens with a comparative concept covering BOTH poems — what both poets do similarly or differently with [form/language/structure] (AO1).
* **Technique Identification — BOTH Poems**: Identifies the specific technique in EACH poem using precise subject terminology (AO2).
* **Evidence — BOTH Poems**: Integrates quotations from BOTH poems smoothly into the prose (AO1/AO2).
* **Comparative Close Analysis**: Zooms in on specific words, sounds, punctuation, or features in BOTH quotes, showing what the comparison reveals at micro-level (AO2).
* **Comparative Effects**: Compares the different effects of each poet's technique on the reader — what each technique makes the reader feel, think, or understand differently (AO2).
* **Comparative Poets' Purposes**: A perceptive insight into WHY each poet made different choices and what the comparison reveals about their different messages or attitudes (AO1/AO2).
* **Sustained Comparative Language**: Uses comparative connectives throughout ("Similarly," "In contrast," "Whereas," "While [Poet A]..., [Poet B]...", "Both poets," etc.).
* **NEVER sequential**: Analysis must weave both poems together — NOT Poem 1 then Poem 2.

**Body Paragraph 1 Focus — FORM Comparison:**
Terminology: narrative poem, lyric, elegy, dramatic monologue, free verse, blank verse, sonnet, ballad, ode, single stanza, regular/irregular stanzas, first/third person, direct address, stream of consciousness.

**Body Paragraph 2 Focus — LANGUAGE Comparison:**
Terminology: imagery (visual, auditory, tactile), metaphor, simile, personification, alliteration, sibilance, assonance, plosives, semantic field, diction, tone, irony, symbolism, onomatopoeia.

**Body Paragraph 3 Focus — STRUCTURE Comparison:**
Terminology: enjambment, caesura, end-stopping, rhyme scheme, metre (regular/irregular), volta, cyclical structure, linear progression, repetition/refrain, anaphora, juxtaposition, stanza breaks, line length variation, sentence length variation.

### **Conclusion Criteria (1 mark)**

* **Restated Comparative Thesis**: A brief synthesis restating the comparative argument in fresh language — what the comparison ultimately reveals about the two poets' different approaches to the shared theme. 1-2 sentences maximum. (AO1)
* **No extended quartet** — at 20 marks, the conclusion is a single powerful comparative statement, not a four-part structure.

### **Mark Allocation for Internal Assessment**

**Total: 20 marks, distributed as:**

* **Introduction (Comparative Thesis):** 1 mark
* **Comparative Body Paragraph 1 (Form):** 6 marks
* **Comparative Body Paragraph 2 (Language):** 6 marks
* **Comparative Body Paragraph 3 (Structure):** 6 marks
* **Conclusion (Restated Thesis):** 1 mark

**Comparative Body Paragraph Mark Breakdown (6 marks each):**
1. Comparative concept-led topic sentence (AO1) — Worth: 1.0 mark
2. Technique identification for BOTH poems with terminology (AO2) — Worth: 0.5 marks
3. Evidence from BOTH poems with smooth integration (AO1/AO2) — Worth: 0.5 marks
4. Comparative close analysis of BOTH quotes (AO2) — Worth: 1.0 mark
5. Comparative effects on reader (AO2) — Worth: 1.0 mark
6. Comparative poets' purposes + what comparison reveals (AO1/AO2) — Worth: 1.0 mark
7. Sustained comparative language throughout (AO1/AO2) — Worth: 1.0 mark

## **2.G. Mark Allocation for Internal Assessment (Q12)**

**Total: 15 marks, distributed as:**

* **Introduction:** 1 mark
* **Body Paragraph 1:** 4 marks
* **Body Paragraph 2:** 4 marks
* **Body Paragraph 3:** 4 marks
* **Conclusion:** 2 marks

**Mark Allocation Details:**

**Introduction (1 mark):**
1. Hook and brief thesis establishing the poem's central theme and signposting the argument (AO1) — Worth: 1.0 mark

**[AI_INTERNAL]:** Q12 only has 20 minutes. The introduction should be concise — a strong opening sentence establishing the theme, followed immediately by a brief thesis. No separate theme building sentence needed at this word count.

**Body Paragraphs (4 marks each):**
1. Concept-led topic sentence with clear argumentative claim (AO1) — Worth: 0.5 marks
2. Precise subject terminology identifying the technique (AO2) — Worth: 0.5 marks
3. Strategic quote selection with smooth integration (AO1/AO2) — Worth: 0.5 marks
4. Close analysis zooming in on specific words/sounds/punctuation (AO2) — Worth: 0.75 marks
5. Effect on reader (AO2) — Worth: 0.75 marks
6. Perceptive insight into the poet's purpose (AO1/AO2) — Worth: 1.0 mark

**Note on Body Paragraph Focus:**
- **Body Paragraph 1 (Form):** Form is MANDATORY for Edexcel — no Beginning pathway option.
- **Body Paragraph 2 (Language):** Language techniques focus.
- **Body Paragraph 3 (Structure):** How the poem concludes and what this achieves.

**Conclusion (2 marks):**
1. Restated thesis and controlling concept (AO1) — Worth: 1.0 mark
2. Poet's purpose and/or universal message (AO1) — Worth: 1.0 mark

**[AI_INTERNAL]:** The conclusion for Q12 is briefer than AQA's 4-mark quartet. Students should aim for 2-3 sentences combining the restated thesis with a perceptive final insight.
