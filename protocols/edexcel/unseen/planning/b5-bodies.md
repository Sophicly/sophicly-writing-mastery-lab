**Why the Ending Matters (Brief Pedagogical Note)**

SAY: "Finally, let's plan **Body Paragraph 3: The Ending** — how the poem concludes and what this achieves.

**Key insight:** The ending of a poem is where meaning crystallises. Poets craft their final lines with extreme care because the ending is what the reader takes away. A poem's conclusion might resolve a tension, leave questions unanswered, circle back to the beginning, present a final striking image, or shift tone dramatically.

Understanding WHAT the ending does — and HOW it does it — demonstrates Level 4-6 analytical skill.

This is your most powerful paragraph — it brings your argument to its climax. The strongest essays save their most profound analysis for last."

ASK: "Ready to analyse the ending?

**A)** Yes, let's go **B)** I'm not sure what to look for in the ending — can you help?"

**[AI_INTERNAL]:**
- **If A:** Proceed to Step 1 below.
- **If B:** SAY: "When analysing the ending of a poem, consider: Does the poem RESOLVE or remain OPEN? Does the TONE shift from how it began? Does the final IMAGE surprise, comfort, or unsettle? Does the ending CHANGE how you understand the rest of the poem? Is there a sense of CLOSURE or does the poem feel deliberately unfinished? These are all powerful analytical angles. Ready now?" Then proceed to Step 1.

**Step 1: Topic Sentence (T)**

SAY: "Let's plan **Body Paragraph 3: The Ending** — how the poem concludes and what this achieves.

- Does it RESOLVE the tension established earlier, or leave it OPEN?
- Does the TONE shift from the rest of the poem?
- Is the final IMAGE striking, surprising, or ambiguous?
- Does the ending CIRCLE BACK to the beginning (cyclical)?
- Does the last line/word carry particular WEIGHT or SIGNIFICANCE?
- Does the ending CHANGE how you understand the rest of the poem?

What is the poet doing at the end of this poem, and why?"

**Wait for the student's response before continuing.**

**[AI_INTERNAL — Ending Identification Validation & Scaffolding]:**

**If student identifies correctly:**

**If student is unsure:** Apply progressive scaffolding:

1. **LEVEL 1 — Reframe:** "Let's approach this differently. Read the very last line of the poem. What is the final WORD? What is the final IMAGE? Why did the poet choose to END here, with THIS?"

2. **LEVEL 2 — Binary:** "Would you say the ending (A) resolves or completes something, or (B) leaves something open or unresolved?"

3. **LEVEL 3 — Multiple Choice:** "Which best describes what the ending does?
   - A) **Resolves** — brings closure, answers the poem's central question
   - B) **Remains open** — leaves questions unanswered, creates ambiguity
   - C) **Shifts tone** — the ending feels different from the rest of the poem
   - D) **Circles back** — returns to the beginning, creating a cyclical structure
   - E) **Presents a final striking image** — one powerful image lingers with the reader
   - F) **Transforms meaning** — the ending changes how you understand everything before it"

4. **LEVEL 4 — Direct Guidance:** "Looking at the final lines, I can see the poet [specific observation]. What do you think this achieves?"

**Follow-up:** "How does this ending BUILD ON everything you've discussed in Body 1 and Body 2? How do [beginning/form], language, and ending work TOGETHER?"

**Wait for the student's response before continuing.**
