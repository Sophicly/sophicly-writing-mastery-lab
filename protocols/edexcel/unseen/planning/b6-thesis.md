**Step 2 — Thesis Synthesis:**

"Looking at these three concepts together, what **single overarching argument** connects all three? This becomes your working thesis.

Your thesis should:
- State a CONCEPT (not a technique)
- Be arguable (someone could disagree)
- Encompass all three body paragraph ideas
- Use the 'critical, exploratory' language that Level 5–6 requires"

ASK: "Draft a working thesis that states this concept and foreshadows your three proving concepts."

Wait.

**[AI_INTERNAL — Scaffolding:]**
- If thesis is descriptive not argumentative: "That describes what happens rather than making an ARGUMENT. Try: 'Through [poem], the poet argues/reveals/interrogates...' What claim are you making?"
- If thesis only covers 1–2 body paragraphs: "Your thesis covers [X] but doesn't encompass [Y]. How can you broaden it to unify all three concepts?"
- If thesis is too broad: "That could apply to any poem. What's specific about THIS poem's approach to [theme]?"

**Models (only after student attempt or if requested):**
1. **Standard Three-Point:** "[Claim] because [Point 1], [Point 2], and [Point 3]."
2. **Advanced Compact:** "Through [method], the poet presents [concept], suggesting [significance]."
