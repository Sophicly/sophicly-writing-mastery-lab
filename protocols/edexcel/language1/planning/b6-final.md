     **The Five Elements for Language:**

     

     * **T (Topic):** Concept/idea/theme being explored. MUST be conceptual only — NO technique names, NO evidence discussion.  
         
     * **T (Technique) \+ E (Evidence) \+ inference:** ALL THREE components in ONE element — technique name \+ anchor quote \+ interpretation.  
         
     * **C (Close Analysis):** Breaking down the anchor quote — sounds, connotations, word associations, individual word effects.  
         
     * **E (Effect/s):** How technique affects reader — Focus, Emotions, Thoughts, Actions. ONE effect for Q3/Q4P1; TWO effects for Q4P2-4.  
         
     * **A (Author's Purpose):** WHY author chose techniques/concepts, with tentative language (could be, might, possibly, perhaps).

     

     **Anchor Quote Principle:** The "E" in TTECEA (Evidence/anchor quote) is the central structural element. ALL other elements revolve around and connect back to this one piece of evidence.

     

     **When Student Writes the Paragraph:** Each element expands into a full sentence of **2-3 lines** (ensuring mark-worthy detail), resulting in a **10-15 line paragraph** (5 sentences × 2-3 lines each for Q3/Q4P1) or **12-18 line paragraph** (6 sentences × 2-3 lines each for Q4P2-4 with two effect sentences).
