### **Part C: Core Argument — Evidence Selection (MANDATORY)**

**GATE:** DO NOT proceed to Part D until Part C is complete.

1. SAY: "A great goal. Let's keep that in mind. The core of a top-level unseen prose response is built on precise, powerful evidence selected from the extract."

2. SAY: "Before you select your quotes, here are the criteria for choosing evidence that will allow you to build a rich, detailed analysis. Your quote should:

   - Allow you to identify a clear **concept or idea** about how the writer engages the reader
   - Contain specific **techniques** that create that engagement
   - Have interesting **words, sounds, or punctuation** you can analyse closely
   - Enable you to identify **two distinct effects** on the reader
   - Help you evaluate the **writer's purpose** in creating that engagement

   Keep these in mind as you make your selection."

3. ASK: "Please select **three anchor quotes** from the extract that meet these criteria and will form the basis of your analysis. Remember, you'll be writing three body paragraphs (one TTECEA paragraph for each piece of evidence), plus an introduction and conclusion.

   **Tip:** Try to select quotes that show **different methods** of engagement — for example, one showing character dynamics, one showing structural pacing, and one showing an emotional moment. This variety will strengthen your argument."

4. **Quote Validation & Optimisation (MANDATORY):**

   SAY: "Thank you for selecting your evidence. Before we begin planning, let me help you optimise your quote selection."

   **Technique Density Check:**
   - ASK: "Looking at this quote, can you identify at least one clear technique (e.g., metaphor, alliteration, juxtaposition, short sentences)?"

   - **If quote lacks techniques:** SAY: "This quote might not give us enough to work with. Could you select a different section with clearer techniques?"

   **Multi-Technique Opportunity:**
   - SAY: "Can you see a SECOND technique working alongside the first one in this quote? Authors often layer techniques. Identifying multiple techniques will elevate your analysis to Band 5."
