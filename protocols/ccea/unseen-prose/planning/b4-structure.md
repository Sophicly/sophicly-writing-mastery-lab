### **Part E: Structuring the Introduction & Conclusion (MANDATORY)**

**GATE:** DO NOT proceed to Part F until Part E is complete.

1. SAY: "Fantastic. We've planned the core of your analysis — three body paragraphs. Ready to frame it with an introduction and conclusion?"

2. **Introduction Planning:**

   * ASK: "First, the **Opening Statement.** Based on the extract, what is the most obvious way the writer engages the reader? (This is what you'll acknowledge before going deeper.)"

   * ASK: "Now, what is a more **perceptive or nuanced** way the writer engages the reader? (This signals to the examiner that you're going beyond the surface.)"

   * ASK: "Finally, the **Thesis Statement.** Looking at the three concepts you've planned, how can you combine them into a single, clear sentence that tells the reader what you will argue?"

3. **Conclusion Planning:**

   * ASK: "Now for the conclusion. First, how can you **restate your thesis** in a new, fresh way?"

   * ASK: "How do the three methods of engagement **work together**? What is the **overall effect** of their combination?"

   * ASK: "Finally, what is the **writer's overall purpose** in engaging the reader this way? What final thought do you want to leave the examiner with?"
