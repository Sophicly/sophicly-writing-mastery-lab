# BOARD FORMAT MODULE — SQA National 5 English

**Board:** SQA National 5 English
**Paper:** Paper 2 — Critical Reading
**Sections:** Section 1 (Scottish Text — 20 marks) | Section 2 (Critical Essay — 20 marks)
**Format:** Extract + 4-5 analysis questions + comparison question

---

## QUESTION FORMAT RULES — NON-NEGOTIABLE

### Section 1: Scottish Text

SQA Scottish Text questions are COMPLETELY different from all English boards. There is NO single essay question. Instead:

1. **A printed extract** from the set text (poem, prose extract, or drama extract)
2. **4-5 short analysis questions** (2-4 marks each) testing specific skills:
   - Understanding (what happens / what is meant)
   - Analysis of language (word choice, imagery, sentence structure, tone)
   - Evaluation (how effective is a technique)
3. **One final comparison question** (8 marks) asking the student to compare the extract with another text by the same writer

**Total: 20 marks** for the Scottish Text section.

### The Comparison Question (8 marks) — Most Important

The final question is always worth 8 marks and follows this format:
- *"With close reference to this [poem/extract] and at least one other [poem/text] by [author], show how [author] explores the theme of [theme]."*
- OR: *"By referring to this [poem/extract] and at least one other [poem/text] by [author], discuss how [author] presents [theme/idea]."*

This is the highest-value single question in the section.

---

## COMPLETE QUESTION STRUCTURE — Poetry

1. **The poem** — printed in full (or relevant section if very long)
2. **Questions 1-4** (2-4 marks each):
   - Q1: Understanding question (e.g. "Explain what the poet means by...")
   - Q2: Language analysis (e.g. "Analyse how the poet's use of word choice in lines X-Y...")
   - Q3: Technique analysis (e.g. "Explain how the poet uses imagery to convey...")
   - Q4: Evaluation or further analysis
3. **Question 5** (8 marks): Comparison question — *"With close reference to this poem and at least one other poem by [poet], show how [poet] explores the theme of [theme]."*

## COMPLETE QUESTION STRUCTURE — Prose

1. **The extract** — a passage from the novel (~1-2 pages)
2. **Questions 1-4** (2-4 marks each):
   - Understanding, language analysis, character analysis, technique
3. **Question 5** (8 marks): Comparison — *"By referring to this extract and elsewhere in the novel, show how [author] develops the theme of [theme]."*

## COMPLETE QUESTION STRUCTURE — Drama

1. **The extract** — a scene or part of a scene
2. **Questions 1-4** (2-4 marks each):
   - Understanding, dialogue analysis, stage directions, character
3. **Question 5** (8 marks): Comparison with the rest of the play

---

## KEY SQA DIFFERENCES

1. **NOT an essay question** — SQA uses short analytical questions, not essay-style responses
2. **Specific line references** — questions point to exact lines (e.g. "In lines 3-4...")
3. **Skill-specific questions** — each question targets a specific analytical skill
4. **The 8-mark comparison** is where students show depth — they must link the extract to other texts by the same author
5. **Scottish texts only** — MacCaig, Duffy, Morgan, Kay (poetry); Jenkins, Robertson, Stevenson, Crichton Smith (prose); Munro, Spence, Di Mambro (drama)

## EXAMPLE OUTPUT — Poetry (Norman MacCaig, "Visiting Hour")

Read the poem below and then answer the questions that follow.

**Visiting Hour** — Norman MacCaig

> The hospital smell
> combs my nostrils
> as they go bobbing along
> green and yellow corridors.
>
> What seems a corpse
> is trundled into a lift and vanishes
> heavenward.
>
> I will not feel, I will not
> feel, until
> I have to.
>
> Nurses walk lightly, swiftly,
> here and there,
> their feet sensibly tiptoe
> on the linoleum.
> Ward 7. She lies
> in a white cave of forgetfulness.
>
> A withered hand
> trembles on its stalk. Eyes move
> behind eyelids too heavy
> to raise. Into an arm washed
> the colour of water
> a glass fang is fixed,
> not guzzling but giving.
>
> And water
> Is in her too,
> Water, Water
> everywhere, Water
> of a kind.

**Questions**

**1.** Look at lines 1-4. Explain what the poet's use of the word "combs" suggests about the hospital smell. (2 marks)

**2.** Look at lines 5-7. Analyse how the poet uses word choice and/or imagery to convey the atmosphere of the hospital. (4 marks)

**3.** Look at lines 8-10. What do these lines reveal about the speaker's emotional state? (2 marks)

**4.** Look at lines 16-24. Analyse how the poet uses imagery to describe the patient. You should refer to two examples in your answer. (4 marks)

**5.** With close reference to this poem and at least one other poem by Norman MacCaig, show how MacCaig explores the theme of loss or suffering. (8 marks)

**Total: 20 marks**

## EXAMPLE OUTPUT — Prose (The Cone Gatherers)

Read the extract below and then answer the questions that follow.

*In this extract, Duror watches Calum gathering cones in the trees.*

> [Extract from The Cone Gatherers — ~20 lines describing Duror's observation of Calum's agility in the trees and his growing resentment]

**Questions**

**1.** Using your own words, explain what Duror is thinking as he watches Calum. (2 marks)

**2.** Analyse how Jenkins uses word choice in this extract to convey Duror's feelings towards Calum. (4 marks)

**3.** Explain what the contrast between Calum's behaviour and Duror's thoughts suggests about their characters. (3 marks)

**4.** Analyse how Jenkins uses sentence structure in this extract to build tension. (3 marks)

**5.** By referring to this extract and elsewhere in the novel, show how Jenkins develops the theme of good versus evil. (8 marks)

**Total: 20 marks**

## GENERATING SQA QUESTIONS — GUIDELINES

When creating SQA-style questions:
- Questions must target SPECIFIC lines (use line references)
- Each question tests a DIFFERENT skill (don't repeat "word choice" twice)
- Skills to rotate through: word choice, imagery, sentence structure, tone, contrast, characterisation, setting, mood
- The 8-mark comparison question should name a THEME that connects to other texts by the same author
- For poetry: use the thematic connections from the past paper data (e.g. MacCaig: loss, nature, humanity; Duffy: identity, love, transformation)
- Mark distribution should total 20 marks (typically: 2+4+2+4+8 or 2+3+3+4+8)
