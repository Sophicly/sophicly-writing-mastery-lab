## **1.A Assessment Objectives & Mark Distribution**

| AO | Description |
|----|-------------|
| **AO1** | Read, understand and respond. Maintain a critical style. Develop an informed personal response. Use textual references, including quotations, to support and illustrate interpretations. |
| **AO2** | Analyse the language, form and structure used by a writer to create meanings and effects, using relevant subject terminology where appropriate. |
| **AO3** | NOT ASSESSED in Section A poetry. |
| **AO4** | NOT ASSESSED in Section A poetry. |

**Mark Distributions:**

| Question | AO1 (% GCSE) | AO2 (% GCSE) | Total |

## **1.B OCR Six-Level Mark Scheme Descriptors**

### **Part (a) — AO2 DOMINANT**

| Level | Marks | AO2 | AO1 | Comparison |
|-------|-------|-----|-----|------------|
| **6** | 18–20 | Detailed and sensitive analysis of language, form and structure. Consistently effective subject terminology. | Coherent critical style, consistently perceptive understanding. Quotes precise, pertinent, skilfully interwoven. | Sustained, interwoven comparison. |
| **5** | 15–17 | Thoughtful examination of language, form and structure. Good subject terminology. | Convincing critical style, insightful understanding. Quotes well-selected and fully integrated. | Sustained comparison. |
| **4** | 11–14 | Some analytical comments on language, form and structure. Competent subject terminology. | Some critical style, clear understanding. Relevant quotes selected. | Some key points of comparison developed. |
| **3** | 7–10 | Reasonable explanation of language, form and structure. Some subject terminology. | Reasonably detailed personal response, shows understanding. Some relevant quotes. | Some explicit, relevant comparisons. |
| **2** | 4–6 | Simple comments on language, form or structure. Limited terminology. | Straightforward personal response, some understanding. Some relevant support. | Some identification of key links. |
| **1** | 1–3 | Little awareness of language, form or structure. Very little terminology. | Few relevant comments. Limited references. | Limited attempt at links. |
| **0** | 0 | No response or no response worthy of credit. | — | — |

**CRITICAL OCR PENALTY:** Only one poem considered → unlikely to exceed Level 2, cannot exceed Level 3.

### **Part (b) — AO1 AND AO2 EQUALLY WEIGHTED**

| Level | Marks | AO2 | AO1 |
|-------|-------|-----|-----|
| **6** | 18–20 | Detailed and sensitive analysis of language, form and structure. Consistently effective terminology. | Coherent critical style, perceptive understanding. Quotes precise, pertinent, interwoven. Sustained, deeply engaged personal interpretation. |
| **5** | 15–17 | Thoughtful examination of language, form and structure. Good terminology. | Convincing critical style, insightful understanding. Quotes well-selected and integrated. |
| **4** | 11–14 | Some analytical comments. Competent terminology. | Some critical style, clear understanding. Relevant quotes selected. |
| **3** | 7–10 | Reasonable explanation. Some terminology. | Reasonably detailed response. Some relevant quotes. |
| **2** | 4–6 | Simple comments. Limited terminology. | Straightforward response. Some support. |
| **1** | 1–3 | Little awareness. Very little terminology. | Few relevant comments. Limited references. |
| **0** | 0 | No response worthy of credit. | — |

### **Mark Calibration Guide**

| Descriptor | Award |
|------------|-------|
| Borderline — this level and the one below | Bottom of level |
| Just enough on balance | Above bottom / at or below middle |
| Meets criteria with some inconsistency | Above middle / at or below top |
| Consistently meets criteria | Top of level |

## **1.C OCR Poetry Clusters Reference**

Three clusters assessed in Section A. Students study ONE. The exam prints a poem beside each cluster question; students compare this with their anthology poem in Part (a), then write about a different anthology poem in Part (b).

| Cluster | Core Theme | Typical Period Range |
|---------|------------|---------------------|
| **Love and Relationships** | Romantic love, self-love, devotion, separation, domestic love | Renaissance → Contemporary |
| **Conflict** | Military, political, racial, personal, social conflict | Various historical periods |
| **Youth and Age** | Generational contrast, innocence vs experience, social attitudes to age | Various historical periods |

**[AI_INTERNAL] Key confirmation at session start:**
1. Which cluster?
2. Which question (Part a / Part b / both)?
3. Which specific anthology poem(s)?

## **1.D Penalty Codes Reference**

**[AI_INTERNAL]** Apply during Assessment Protocol A. Surface constructively — explain WHY the penalty limits the mark.

| Code | Name | Deduction | Detection |
|------|------|-----------|-----------|
| **N1** | Narrative retelling | −1 per instance | Describes what happens without analytical comment |
| **N2** | Unsupported assertion | −1 per instance | Analytical claim without textual evidence |
| **W1** | Weak analytical verb | −0.5 per instance | "shows," "tells us," "is about" |
| **S1** | Single-poem drift (Part a) | −2 per paragraph | Body paragraph in Part (a) analyses only one poem |
| **S2** | No comparison (Part a) | −3 total | Essay treats poems sequentially throughout |
| **Q1** | Hanging quote | −0.5 per instance | Quote dropped in without grammatical integration |
| **Q2** | Wrong-cluster poem (Part b) | Disqualify | Part (b) uses poem from different cluster |
| **Q3** | Part (a) poem reused in Part (b) | −3 | Same poem used in both questions |
| **F1** | Form/Structure confusion | −0.5 per instance | Structural feature labelled as form (or vice versa) |
| **T1** | No subject terminology | −1 per paragraph | No literary terms in the paragraph |

Surface penalties: "I noticed [issue] here — this can limit your mark because [reason]. Here's how we address it..."
