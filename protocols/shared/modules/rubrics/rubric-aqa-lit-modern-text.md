# Rubric — AQA GCSE Literature, Paper 2 Section A (Modern Texts)

**Loads on top of:** `rubric-base.md`. Universal Sophicly rules apply unless overridden here.

**Used by:** `inline-coaching-engine-1.md` for tasks `task='exam_crib'` (An Inspector Calls / Blood Brothers / Animal Farm / Lord of the Flies / Anita and Me / Pigeon English / Leave Taking) + `task='polishing'` (AQA Lit P2 Section A redraft).

**Paper structure:** Single 45-minute essay-only question (no extract). 30 marks (AO1+AO2+AO3) + 4 marks AO4 (SPaG) = 34 total.

---

## ASSESSMENT OBJECTIVES + WEIGHTING (PAPER 2 SECTION A)

| AO | Marks | Description |
|---|---:|---|
| AO1 | ~12 | Read, understand, respond + textual references. |
| AO2 | ~12 | Analyse language / form / structure for meaning + effect. |
| AO3 | ~6 | Context — relationships between text and contexts. |
| AO4 | 4 | SPaG accuracy. |

**Critical:** AO4 IS SPaG for modern texts (same as Shakespeare). Different from 19c novel (no AO4).

**Pointer phrase:** *AQA modern text essays carry AO4 SPaG marks (4 marks) on top of the 30-mark analytical core.*

---

## QUESTION SHAPE — NO EXTRACT

> How does [author] present [X] in [text]? Write about: how [author] presents [X]; how [author] uses [Y] to present [X]. **[30 marks] + [4 marks AO4]**

**Critical difference from Paper 1:** NO extract is provided. Students bring their own quotes from memory. Quote-bank prep is essential.

**Pointer phrase:** *Paper 2 Section A has no extract — quote-bank memory is the load-bearing skill.*

## PER-TEXT LOAD-BEARING SCENES / CHAPTERS

### An Inspector Calls (Priestley, 1945 set 1912)
- Act 1: Birling's "unsinkable" Titanic speech / Inspector's arrival.
- Act 2: Sheila's progressive shift / Mrs Birling's refusal of responsibility.
- Act 3: Inspector's "fire and blood and anguish" speech / phone call reveal.

### Blood Brothers (Russell)
- Act 1: Mrs Johnstone's bargain / Mickey vs Edward childhood.
- Act 2: separation widens / class divergence.
- Act 2 ending: dual deaths / Narrator's "did you ever hear the tale of the Johnstone twins?".

### Animal Farm (Orwell, 1945)
- Ch 1: Old Major's vision.
- Ch 7: Boxer's "I will work harder."
- Ch 10: pigs walking on two legs / "all animals are equal but some are more equal than others."

### Lord of the Flies (Golding, 1954)
- Ch 1: conch + Ralph elected.
- Ch 4: Jack's hunters paint faces.
- Ch 8: Lord of the Flies addresses Simon.
- Ch 12: rescue + Ralph's grief for "the end of innocence."

### Anita and Me (Syal, 1996)
- Meena's narrative voice + Tollington setting.
- Anita's bullying / racial dimension.
- Cultural-bridging ending.

### Leave Taking (Pinnock)
- Mai's spiritual practice as cultural anchor.
- Generational language-tension between mother + daughters.

## CONTEXT (AO3) — POST-1914 ANCHORS

### An Inspector Calls
- *Set 1912 / written 1945* — Priestley's didactic gap.
- *Two World Wars between writing and setting* — refutes Birling's optimism.
- *Beveridge Report 1942* — welfare-state context.
- *Socialist 1945 Labour landslide* — Priestley's audience.
- *Theatre of moral instruction* — morality-play tradition.

### Blood Brothers
- *1981 Liverpool* — Thatcherism / unemployment context.
- *Class as fate* — nature-vs-nurture central tension.

### Animal Farm
- *1945 publication* / *Russian Revolution 1917 + Stalin's USSR* — direct allegory.
- *Beasts of England* echoes *Internationale*.
- *"Some animals more equal"* echoes *Animal Farm*'s real-world parallel in Soviet purges.

### Lord of the Flies
- *1954 publication* / post-WW2 / nuclear anxiety.
- *Hobbes Leviathan 1651* — state-of-nature tradition.
- *Boys' adventure tradition (Coral Island 1857)* — Golding's ironic inversion.
- *Holocaust as proximal cause* of Golding's loss-of-faith-in-civilisation.

### Anita and Me
- *Late 1960s / early 1970s Tollington* — pre-multicultural era setting.
- *Enoch Powell "Rivers of Blood" 1968* — racial-tension context.
- *Female Bildungsroman tradition*.

**Pointer phrase:** *Modern-text AO3 lands strongest when the gap between SETTING date and PUBLICATION date is the analytical lever (especially AIC, Animal Farm).*

## CRITICAL FRAMES — PER-TEXT

Stack on top of base critical frames:

- **AIC:** Priestley as didactic / theatre-of-moral-instruction.
- **Animal Farm:** Allegorical reading (Stalinism) + reader-as-betrayed-revolutionary frame.
- **Lord of the Flies:** Hobbesian state-of-nature reading + ironic inversion of Coral Island.
- **Blood Brothers:** Class-as-fate reading; nature-vs-nurture as social-determinist tragedy.

---

## MODERN-TEXT-SPECIFIC ANTI-PATTERNS

(Stack on top of base anti-patterns.)

- **Treating set-date as publication-date.** AIC's lever IS the gap. Same with Animal Farm.
- **Birling = "wrong about everything" only.** Better as Priestley's *device* — capitalist-archetype audience must reject.
- **Inspector Goole as "real police."** He is allegorical / supernatural / Priestley-vehicle. Underline.
- **Boxer as just "stupid."** He is loyalty-as-virtue exploited; Orwell's most painful indictment of revolutionary betrayal.
- **Generic "society is bad" themes** without paper-specific historical anchor.

## CONTROLLING-CONCEPT EXEMPLARS
