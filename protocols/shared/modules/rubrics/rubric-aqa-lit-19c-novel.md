## CONTEXT (AO3) — VICTORIAN / ROMANTIC ANCHORS

Strong per-text anchors:

### Frankenstein (Romantic / Gothic)
- *Galvanism / Aldini Newgate 1803* — animation context.
- *Villa Diodati 1816* — composition context.
- *Year-without-summer / Tambora 1815-16* — atmospheric context.
- *Wollstonecraft death 1797* / *A Vindication of the Rights of Woman 1792* — feminist mother (FRAME AS Romantic-period social-vulnerability, not feminist critique per `feedback_avoid_feminist_critique_framework`).
- *Godwin Political Justice 1793* — radical philosophical lineage.
- *Rousseau Émile 1762* / *Locke 1690* — education-of-the-mind philosophy.
- *Paradise Lost 1667* — creator-creature parallel.
- *Industrial Revolution + Luddite riots* — fear-of-machinery context.
- *Coleridge Ancient Mariner 1798* — guilt-and-pursuit Gothic frame.

### Jane Eyre (Bildungsroman with Gothic devices)
- *Bildungsroman tradition* — Goethe Wilhelm Meister 1795-96.
- *1847 publication* — Brontë's pseudonym Currer Bell.
- *Coverture marriage law* — legal frame for Rochester proposal (per `feedback_no_sexual_references_in_student_materials` — reframe as legal-status, not sexual).
- *Governess vulnerability* — social-status, not sexual.
- *Wollstonecraft 1792* — Enlightenment moral-equality argument.
- *Romanticism* — Red Room sublime-terror tradition.

### A Christmas Carol (Victorian social critique)
- *1843 publication* — middle of Hungry Forties.
- *Malthus 1798 An Essay on the Principle of Population* — Scrooge's "decrease the surplus population" line.
- *Poor Law 1834* — workhouse context.
- *Ignorance & Want* (Stave 3) — Dickens' direct attack on Victorian neglect.
- *Carlyle Past and Present 1843* — same-year social-conscience companion.

### Jekyll & Hyde (late-Victorian Gothic)
- *1886 publication* — fin-de-siècle.
- *Darwin Origin of Species 1859* / *Descent of Man 1871* — atavism / regression fears.
- *Lombroso born-criminal 1876* — physiognomy / criminology.
- *Freud subconscious* (anachronistically anticipated by Stevenson, OK to reference).
- *Late-Victorian fear of own hypocrisy / hidden self* (per memory `feedback_gothic_as_psychological_societal_fears`).

### Pride & Prejudice (Regency social comedy)
- *1813 publication*.
- *Burke Reflections on the Revolution in France 1790* — conservative tradition Austen sits within.
- *Wollstonecraft 1792* — counter-voice, education-of-women debate.
- *Entail / primogeniture* — legal frame for Mr Bennet's predicament.
- *Free indirect discourse* — Austen's narrative innovation.

### Sign of the Four (1890 Conan Doyle)
- *1890 publication* — second Holmes novel.
- *British Empire / India / 1857 Mutiny* — Tonga / treasure backstory.
- *Late-Victorian crime fiction tradition*.
- *Cocaine respectability* — pre-1914 medical context.

**Pointer phrase:** *AO3 for 19c novel lands strongest with date + named source linked to specific BP claim.*

## CRITICAL FRAMES — PER-TEXT

Stack on top of base critical frames (Bradley / Eagleton / Ryan / Smith):

- **Frankenstein:** Romantic individualism critique, science-without-conscience reading, parental abandonment frame.
- **Jane Eyre:** Bildungsroman moral-formation frame, Gothic-device-inside-realist-novel.
- **ACC:** Carol-as-allegory frame, Dickens as social-conscience novelist.
- **J&H:** Gothic doubling tradition, late-Victorian self-as-stranger anxiety.
- **P&P:** Free indirect discourse + Burkean reading, comedy-of-manners-as-social-critique.
- **SoF:** detective-fiction-as-Empire-anxiety reading.

**Banned framing per memory:** no "patriarchy" / "feminist critique" / "magic" — see `rubric-base.md`.

## 19c-NOVEL-SPECIFIC ANTI-PATTERNS

(Stack on top of base anti-patterns.)

- **Hamartia for non-tragedy texts.** Hamartia is a tragedy term — applies to Macbeth, NOT to 19c novels. Don't force the term onto Frankenstein / Jane Eyre / Scrooge.
- **"Frankenstein is the monster."** A common student conflation. Frankenstein = the scientist; the creature is unnamed. Flag.
- **Scrooge as just "miser."** Reductive. He is also "solitary as an oyster" — Dickens frames isolation as the engine of cruelty.
- **Darcy's pride as character flaw only.** Better as social-class conditioning critiqued through Elizabeth's progressive recognition.
- **Sexualised governess / coverture readings.** Reframe as legal-vulnerability per memory `feedback_no_sexual_references_in_student_materials`.
- **Generic "Gothic" framings** without society-specific psychological fear (per memory `feedback_gothic_as_psychological_societal_fears`).
