# Rubric — AQA GCSE Literature, Paper 2 Section C (Unseen Poetry)

**Loads on top of:** `rubric-base.md`. Universal Sophicly rules apply unless overridden here.

**Used by:** `inline-coaching-engine-1.md` for tasks `task='exam_crib'` (unseen-poetry practice) + `task='polishing'` (AQA Lit P2 Section C redraft).

**Paper structure:** TWO unseen poems. Q27.1 = single-poem analysis (24 marks AO1+AO2). Q27.2 = comparison of methods (8 marks AO2). NO AO3 anywhere. NO AO4.

---

## ASSESSMENT OBJECTIVES + WEIGHTING

### Q27.1 — Single-poem analysis (24 marks)

| AO | Marks | Description |
|---|---:|---|
| AO1 | ~12 | Read, understand, respond + textual references. |
| AO2 | ~12 | Analyse language / form / structure. |
| AO3 | — | NOT assessed. |
| AO4 | — | NOT assessed. |

### Q27.2 — Comparison of methods (8 marks)

| AO | Marks | Description |
|---|---:|---|
| AO1 | — | NOT assessed in Q27.2. |
| AO2 | 8 | Compare methods (specifically) used by both poets. |
| AO3 | — | NOT assessed. |
| AO4 | — | NOT assessed. |

**Pointer phrase:** *Q27.1 = single-poem analysis (no context). Q27.2 = pure method comparison (8 marks AO2 only).*

---

## QUESTION SHAPE

### Q27.1
> In '[Poem 1]', how does the poet present [X]? **[24 marks]**

Single-poem analysis. NO comparison required. NO context expected.

### Q27.2
> In both '[Poem 1]' and '[Poem 2]', the poets describe [X]. What are the similarities and / or differences between the methods the poets use to present [X]? **[8 marks]**

Pure METHOD comparison. Reuse of Poem 1 from Q27.1 is **penalised** (-3 marks for sequential treatment). Use a DIFFERENT angle on Poem 1 in Q27.2.

**Critical rule (per memory + per protocol audit):** Q27.2 penalises reusing Q27.1 angle (-3). Penalises sequential single-poem treatment (-3).

**Pointer phrase:** *Q27.2 demands fresh angles + comparative method analysis — no recycled Q27.1 material.*

---

## LEVEL DESCRIPTORS

### Q27.1 (24 marks)

- **Level 4 (clear):** clear analysis, explained methods, some textual support.
- **Level 5 (thoughtful, developed):** thoughtful analysis, effective method exploration, developed explanation of effects.
- **Level 6 (critical, exploratory):** critical, exploratory, conceptualised reading; precise method analysis; convincing exploration of effects.

### Q27.2 (8 marks)

- **Level 3 (clear):** clear comparison of methods, similar/different identified.
- **Level 4 (thoughtful):** thoughtful comparison of methods + their effects.

**Pointer phrase:** *Q27.2 caps at Level 4 — focused method-comparison earns the marks; sequential treatment caps at Level 3.*

---

## TTECEA SHAPE — ADAPTED FOR UNSEEN POETRY

### Q27.1 — Single poem (full TTECEA without context)

1. T (concept-only).
2. T+e (technique + quote, anchored line ref).
3. E1 (reader reaction 1).
4. C (close analysis — words inside quote).
5. E2 (reader reaction 2).
6. A (poet's purpose — argued, not described).
7. **NO C (Context)** — AO3 not assessed for unseen.

### Q27.2 — Comparison of methods

Folded comparative TTECEA:

1. T (concept — applies to both poems).
2. T+e (method + quote, POEM 1).
3. T+e (method + quote, POEM 2 — distinct angle from Q27.1 if same poem).
4. C (close analysis — words inside both quotes, comparing).
5. E (combined effect across poems).
6. A (poets' purposes — compared).

(Q27.2 essays typically 1-2 BPs only — 8 marks doesn't reward 4 BPs.)

---

## NO AO3 — UNSEEN-SPECIFIC RULE

Q27.1 + Q27.2 are pure language / form / structure analysis. **Do not import biographical or historical context** — there are no marks for it, and it wastes word count.

Common student mistake: treating unseen like anthology + adding "the Romantic-era context here..." when no AO3 marks exist.

**Pointer phrase:** *Unseen poetry = no context. All marks for AO1 + AO2 (and Q27.2 = AO2-only).*

---

## METHOD VOCABULARY (LOAD-BEARING FOR Q27.2)

Specific methods to name + analyse:

- **Form:** sonnet / dramatic monologue / free verse / ballad / ode / villanelle / pantoum / ghazal.
- **Structure:** stanzaic / volta / refrain / enjambment / caesura / chiasmus / non-linear / framed.
- **Language:** specific imagery / sound devices (alliteration / assonance / sibilance) / syntactic patterns (anaphora / polyptoton / antithesis / triadic structure).
- **Tone / register:** confessional / elegiac / sardonic / detached / impassioned.

**Pointer phrase:** *Method comparison demands specific named methods, not "the poet uses imagery."*

---

## METHOD-VS-THEME DISTINCTION

Q27.2 specifically rewards method comparison, NOT theme comparison. If a comparison sentence describes WHAT both poems argue (theme) without naming HOW they make the argument (method), it does not earn AO2 marks.

**Bad (theme-only):** *"Both poems present grief as overwhelming."*

**Good (method comparison):** *"Both poems use enjambment to mirror grief's refusal to settle into stanzaic order."*

**Pointer phrase:** *Q27.2 method-comparison: HOW the poets make the point, not WHAT the point is.*

---

## UNSEEN-SPECIFIC ANTI-PATTERNS

(Stack on top of base anti-patterns.)

- **Importing biographical context.** Wastes words; no AO3 marks. Flag.
- **Speculating on dates.** "This poem is probably from the 1800s..." — students don't know, can't infer reliably, and there are no marks.
- **Q27.2 reusing Q27.1 angle on Poem 1** (penalised -3 per protocol audit at `protocols/aqa/unseen/modules/protocol-c-polishing-unseen.md`).
- **Sequential poem-by-poem treatment in Q27.2** (penalised -3, caps at Level 3).
- **Form-naming in topic sentence.** Forms appear in sentence 2 (per `rubric-base.md`).
- **Theme-only comparison in Q27.2.** Method = HOW; Theme = WHAT.
- **Long quotes in unseen.** Time-pressure doesn't reward long quotes; students must select 3-8 words sharply.

## TIMING CALIBRATION (UNSEEN)

Paper 2 has 2h 15m total. Section C (unseen) gets ~30 minutes split: ~22 min Q27.1 + ~8 min Q27.2.

**Pointer phrase:** *Q27.2 is 8 marks for 8 minutes — focused, comparative, ruthlessly trimmed.*

---

## NO CONTROLLING-CONCEPT EXEMPLAR

Unseen has no per-poem corpus to draw from. Students invent the controlling concept on the fly from the text alone. Coaching focus = method-naming + close-analysis + concept-distillation skills.

**Pointer phrase:** *Unseen rewards skill-of-method-analysis itself — there is no exemplar to memorise.*
