# Rubric — Edexcel IGCSE English Language (Spec A, Paper 2)

**Used by:** `inline-coaching-engine-1.md` for tasks `task='exam_crib'` (Edexcel IGCSE Lang texts) + `task='polishing'` (Edexcel IGCSE Lang phase 2 redraft).

**Treat as authoritative reference.** Quote criterion language verbatim during `EQ_PROMPT(focus_area)` calls.

**Scope:** Edexcel IGCSE English Language Spec A — Paper 2 (Reading + Writing). Section A = non-fiction reading comprehension (Q1, Q2, Q3). Section B = directed writing (Q4) + free creative writing (Q5). For Spec B, see separate rubric (TBD).

## CRITICAL DISTINCTION FROM GCSE — AO4 = CONTEXT

**Edexcel IGCSE AO4 = Context (NOT SPaG).** This is the opposite of AQA GCSE Lang where AO4 = SPaG.

Per memory `feedback_dashboard_exercise_display_derivation` + verified at `protocols/edexcel-igcse/language1/modules/knowledge-macros.md:36, 758, 761`. Grep markers: `"AO4 (cohesion, clarity, precision, audience)"` and `"AO4 (27 marks) + AO5 (18 marks)"`.

When pointing at AO4 for IGCSE Spec A, NEVER reference SPaG. Reference cohesion / clarity / precision / audience awareness.

## ASSESSMENT OBJECTIVES + MARK DISTRIBUTION

| AO | Weight (Paper 2) | Description |
|---|---:|---|
| AO1 | 13 marks | Read and understand a variety of texts. Select information. |
| AO2 | 22 marks | Understand and analyse how writers use language and structure to achieve effects. (Q3 is the AO2-heavy Q.) |
| AO3 | 12 marks | Compare different texts (where applicable). |
| AO4 | 27 marks | **Context — cohesion, clarity, precision, audience.** Use vocabulary, sentence structures and language conventions for clarity, purpose, and effect. |
| AO5 | 18 marks | Write clearly, effectively, and imaginatively. Organise information using structural and grammatical features for coherence. |

**Pointer phrases (use verbatim):**
- *Edexcel IGCSE AO4 rewards cohesion, clarity, precision, and audience awareness — context, not SPaG.*
- *AO2 rewards close analysis of how the writer uses language and structure for effect.*
- *AO5 rewards inventive structure + clear coherence in writing.*

## SECTION A — READING (Q1, Q2, Q3)

### Q1 — Information retrieval (lower-mark, AO1)

- Pure information-finding from the source text.
- Short, precise answer expected. NO analysis required.
- **Common error:** over-writing. Q1 does NOT reward analysis — students must answer concisely and move on.
- **Pointer phrase:** *Q1 rewards precise information retrieval. Are you adding analysis the question doesn't ask for?*

### Q2 — Identifying writers' techniques (mid-mark, AO1 + AO2)

- Identify named techniques + brief explanation of effect.
- Quote-anchored.
- 2-3 sentences per identified technique.
- **Pointer phrase:** *Q2 needs technique-named, quote-anchored, effect-explained — three beats per identification.*

### Q3 — Close analysis (high-mark, AO2-heavy: ~22 marks)

- The Section A flagship. Detailed how-the-writer-uses-language-and-structure analysis.
- Multiple TTECEA-style paragraphs (typically 3-4 BPs).
- Each BP: technique identified, evidence quoted, close analysis of words inside the quote, effect on reader articulated.
- **Critical:** Q3 specifically tests AO2 — analysis of methods. Context (AO4) is NOT the focus of Q3 prose; it surfaces in AO5 (writing-for-audience) at Q5 instead.
- **Pointer phrase:** *Q3 is AO2-heavy — 22 marks for close language and structure analysis. Are your sentences anchored in specific words from the quote?*

---

## SECTION B — WRITING (Q4, Q5)

### Q4 — Directed writing (transactional, AO4 + AO5)

- Write for a specific audience and purpose (letter / article / speech / report).
- AO4 rewards audience awareness + cohesion.
- AO5 rewards structural variety + clarity.
- Structure shape: **IUMVCC** (Introduction / Urgent context / Main argument / Voice / Counter / Conclusion) — Sophicly's locked transactional shape (per memory `feedback_iumvcc_explanation_in_booklets`).
- **Pointer phrase:** *Q4 transactional writing rewards IUMVCC shape — Introduction, Urgent context, Main argument, Voice, Counter, Conclusion.*

### Q5 — Free creative writing (AO4 + AO5, ~45 marks combined)

- Open-ended creative prompt. Narrative or descriptive.
- AO5 rewards inventive structure (e.g. non-linear / framed / time-shift).
- AO4 rewards clear cohesion + precise vocabulary + audience-appropriate register.
- **Locked shape (Sophicly):** Scene-Structure for narrative (5-beat: opening / inciting incident / rising tension / climax / resolution) — per memory `feedback_language_fiction_scene_structure_only`.
- **Pointer phrase:** *Q5 narrative rewards Scene Structure — five beats from opening to resolution.*

## LEVEL DESCRIPTORS (UPGRADE LADDER, SPEC A LANG)

Apply to any individual Q's response.

- **Level 3 (clear):** clear understanding, clear method-effect link, some textual support, mostly accurate written communication.
- **Level 4 (effective):** effective analysis, effective use of detail, effective written expression.
- **Level 5 (perceptive, assured):** perceptive interpretation, assured reference to meanings/effects, sensitive and evaluative analysis, sophisticated written expression.

**Pointer phrases (use verbatim):**
- *Level 4 needs effective analysis — method linked to effect.*
- *Level 5 rewards perceptive interpretation + assured reference to meanings.*
- *Level 5 needs sensitive and evaluative analysis.*

---

## TTECEA-STYLE PARAGRAPH SHAPE (Q2, Q3)

For analytical Qs, the locked Sophicly shape applies (same as GCSE TTECEA, glossed for IGCSE):

1. **Topic** — concept the paragraph argues. Concept-only sentence (no techniques named).
2. **Technique + evidence** — names the technique, quotes the line.
3. **Close analysis** — words inside the quote, unpacked. (THIS IS WHERE AO2 LANDS.)
4. **Effect on reader 1** — what the reader feels or thinks.
5. **Effect on reader 2** — a different feeling or thought.
6. **Author's purpose** — what the writer may be arguing. (For Lang, A is **OPTIONAL** per `feedback_authors_purpose_optional_for_language` — failure mode is bolted A as closing-sentence add-on, NOT omitted A.)
7. **(For Lang Q2/Q3:) NO context (AO4) at sentence 7.** AO4 surfaces in writing Qs (Q4/Q5), not in Section A reading Qs.

---

## QUOTE INTEGRATION

Same shape as AQA Lit:
- Embedded in analytical sentence, not appended.
- Short quotes preferred.
- Always pair with line/paragraph reference from source text.

**Pointer phrase:** *AO2 rewards quotes embedded in the analytical sentence.*

---

## VOCABULARY PRECISION — BANNED VERBS

Same as AQA Lit. Banned: `shows`, `is`, `has`, `gets`, `does`. Replace with precise verbs (`illustrates`, `demonstrates`, `emphasises`, `reveals`, etc.).

**Pointer phrase:** *Level 5 rewards precise terminology — vague verbs ("shows") cap analysis at Level 3.*

---

## ACADEMIC REGISTER

Same as AQA Lit: no contractions / colloquialisms / chatty asides / first-person intrusions / exclamation marks / vague hedges.

For Q4 directed writing, register varies by audience (formal letter vs friendly speech). The rubric criterion = appropriateness, not formality-by-default.

**Pointer phrase:** *Q4 rewards register matched to audience. Q3 rewards strict academic register throughout.*

---

## SENTENCE LENGTH CAP

≤ 35-45 words (≤ ~3 lines).

**Pointer phrase:** *Sentences over ~35 words usually carry weight that two short sentences would carry better.*

---

## STRUCTURE RULES

- No arrow characters (→, ->, =>) in essay prose OR plan bullets.
- No form-naming in topic sentence.
- For Q5: the structural device is the marked feature — name it in the plan, not in the topic sentence of the response itself.

---

## ANTI-PATTERNS TO FLAG

- **Bolted-on A at end of Q2/Q3 BP.** Closing-sentence add-on. Lang Q2/Q3 don't reward A; flag for removal or weave.
- **AO4-as-SPaG.** Spec A AO4 = Context. Confusion with GCSE AO4 (=SPaG) leads to mis-self-assessment.
- **Q1 over-writing.** Treating Q1 like Q3 — adding analysis where pure retrieval is asked.
- **Q5 with no inventive structure.** Linear narrative "and then... and then..." caps at Level 3.
- **IUMVCC missing in Q4.** Generic transactional writing without the locked shape.
