# Topic 1: Ralph
**Type:** A-MT
**Teaching Point:** Ralph represents civilisation, order, and democratic leadership. Students should explore how Golding uses Ralph's declining authority to show how fragile civilised behaviour is when removed from the structures that enforce it.
**Marks:** 34
**AOs:** AO1,AO2,AO3
**AO4:** 4

## Question
How does Golding use the character of Ralph to explore ideas about civilisation and order in Lord of the Flies?

Write about:
- what Ralph says and does
- how Golding presents Ralph.

# Topic 6: The Beast
**Type:** A-MT
**Teaching Point:** The Beast is Golding's central symbol for irrational fear and the darkness within humanity. It exists only in the boys' imaginations yet drives real violence and tribal division. Students should analyse how Golding uses the Beast to show that the greatest threat to civilisation comes not from external monsters but from the evil that people project outward to avoid confronting in themselves.
**Marks:** 34
**AOs:** AO1,AO2,AO3
**AO4:** 4

## Question
How does Golding use the Beast to explore ideas about fear and evil in Lord of the Flies?

Write about:
- what the boys say and do in response to the Beast
- how Golding presents the Beast.

# Topic 7: Civilisation vs Savagery
**Type:** A-MT
**Teaching Point:** The central thematic conflict of the novel is the tension between the impulse toward civilisation and the pull toward savagery. Students should explore how Golding uses the conch, the fire, face paint, and the gradual abandonment of rules to present this conflict as one that exists within every individual, not just between opposing groups.
**Marks:** 34
**AOs:** AO1,AO2,AO3
**AO4:** 4

## Question
How does Golding explore the conflict between civilisation and savagery in Lord of the Flies?

Write about:
- how the boys' behaviour changes throughout the novel
- how Golding presents the struggle between civilisation and savagery.

# Topic 8: Power and Leadership
**Type:** A-MT
**Teaching Point:** Golding contrasts democratic and authoritarian models of leadership through Ralph and Jack. Students should analyse how power shifts on the island and what Golding suggests about the methods people use to gain and maintain control, particularly how fear and spectacle prove more effective than reason and fairness.

**AOs:** AO1,AO2,AO3
**AO4:** 4

How does Golding explore ideas about power and leadership in Lord of the Flies?

# Topic 9: Violence and Its Escalation
**Type:** A-MT
**Teaching Point:** Violence in the novel escalates from play-fighting and hunting to ritual killing and murder. Students should trace this progression and analyse how Golding uses it to suggest that once moral boundaries are crossed, each subsequent act of violence becomes easier, showing how quickly a society can descend into brutality.
**Marks:** 34
**AOs:** AO1,AO2,AO3
**AO4:** 4

## Question
How does Golding explore the theme of violence and its escalation in Lord of the Flies?

Write about:
- how violence develops throughout the novel
- how Golding presents violence and its effects.

# Topic 10: The Island as Setting and Symbol
**Type:** A-MT
**Teaching Point:** The island functions both as a realistic setting and as a symbolic microcosm of the wider world. Students should analyse how Golding uses specific locations — the beach, the mountain, Castle Rock, the jungle — to reflect the boys' psychological states and the novel's broader allegory about human civilisation and its fragility.
**Marks:** 34
**AOs:** AO1,AO2,AO3
**AO4:** 4

## Question
How does Golding use the island setting to explore ideas about society and human nature in Lord of the Flies?

Write about:
- how different parts of the island are used in the novel
- how Golding presents the island as a symbol.
