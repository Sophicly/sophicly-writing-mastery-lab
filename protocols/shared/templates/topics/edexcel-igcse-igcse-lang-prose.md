# Topic 3: The Necklace (Guy de Maupassant)
**Type:** single
**Format:** single
**Teaching Point:** Maupassant's story follows Mathilde Loisel's obsession with wealth and appearance, which leads her to borrow a necklace, lose it, and spend a decade in poverty repaying its replacement — only to discover it was worthless. The story's ironic structure and precise, unsentimental prose expose the destructive power of vanity and social aspiration. Students should analyse how Maupassant uses narrative pacing, characterisation, and the devastating final revelation to convey his themes.
**Marks:** 30
**AOs:** AO1, AO2

## Source A
**Title:** The Necklace
**Author:** Guy de Maupassant (1884)
**Context:** 19th century French realist fiction, translated from the original French. Maupassant, a master of the short story form, uses the tale to critique bourgeois values and the illusion of social status in Parisian society.
