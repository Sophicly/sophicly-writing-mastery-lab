# Topic 1: My Grandmother's Houses
**Type:** poetry_named
**Teaching Point:** My Grandmother's Houses by Jackie Kay traces the speaker's childhood memories of visiting two very different grandmothers, using the contrasting domestic spaces to explore identity, belonging, and the complexities of being adopted into a mixed-race family. Students should analyse how Kay uses concrete sensory detail, the child's perspective, and the structural contrast between the two houses to reveal how environment shapes a child's sense of self and security.
**Marks:** 20
**AOs:** KU,Analysis,Evaluation
**Focus Poem:** My Grandmother's Houses
**Focus Poet:** Jackie Kay

## Question

# Topic 3: Lucozade
**Type:** poetry_named
**Teaching Point:** Lucozade by Jackie Kay captures a hospital visit to the speaker's seriously ill mother, transforming the mundane details of grapes, Lucozade, and a hospital ward into a meditation on fear, love, and the desperate human need to offer comfort. Students should analyse how Kay uses the shift in tone from anxiety to tentative hope, the symbolic role of the Lucozade bottle, and the contrast between illness and vitality to explore the emotional intensity of watching a loved one suffer.

**AOs:** KU,Analysis,Evaluation
**Focus Poem:** Lucozade
**Focus Poet:** Jackie Kay

# Topic 5: Old Tongue

# Topic 8: Divorce
**Type:** poetry_named
**Teaching Point:** Divorce by Jackie Kay is written from a child's perspective, using the language and logic of divorce proceedings to describe a child's desire to separate from her parents. The dark humour of a child "divorcing" her mother and father masks genuine pain, confusion, and a need for control in a situation where the child feels powerless. Students should analyse how Kay uses the legal register, the child's voice, and the ironic reversal of adult and child roles to explore family conflict, emotional survival, and the way children process upheaval.
**Marks:** 20
**AOs:** KU,Analysis,Evaluation
**Focus Poem:** Divorce
**Focus Poet:** Jackie Kay

# Topic 10: My Grandmother's Houses (Revisited — Heritage Focus)
**Type:** poetry_named
**Teaching Point:** Returning to My Grandmother's Houses with a heritage-focused lens, students should now explore how the poem dramatises the tension between two competing inheritances — the warmth and chaos of one grandmother's world against the order and reserve of the other. The two houses become symbols of the dual cultural heritage the adopted speaker must navigate. Students should analyse how Kay uses the contrasting imagery of each domestic space, the child's unspoken awareness of racial difference, and the poem's refusal to choose one grandmother over the other to explore how mixed heritage shapes an individual's understanding of home and family.
**Marks:** 20
**AOs:** KU,Analysis,Evaluation
**Focus Poem:** My Grandmother's Houses
**Focus Poet:** Jackie Kay
