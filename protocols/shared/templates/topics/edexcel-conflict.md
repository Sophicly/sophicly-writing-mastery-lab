# Topic 1: Exposure
**Type:** poetry_named
**Teaching Point:** Exposure by Wilfred Owen presents the psychological and physical suffering of soldiers enduring trench warfare in winter. Students should analyse how Owen uses half-rhyme, the haunting refrain "But nothing happens," and the personification of weather as an enemy to argue that the true horror of war lies not in combat but in the relentless, dehumanising exposure to cold, boredom, and despair. The poem's cyclical structure reinforces the futility of their suffering.
**Marks:** 20
**AOs:** AO2,AO3
**Focus Poem:** Exposure
**Focus Poet:** Wilfred Owen

## Question
Compare how poets present the suffering caused by conflict in 'Exposure' and one other poem from the Conflict anthology.

# Topic 16: War Photographer

**Teaching Point:** War Photographer by Carol Ann Duffy examines the moral tension of documenting suffering for a comfortable, detached audience. Students should analyse how Duffy uses the contrast between the darkroom and the war zone, the religious imagery of the photographer as "a priest preparing to intone a Mass," and the tightly controlled stanzas to explore guilt, desensitisation, and the unbridgeable gap between those who witness conflict firsthand and those who glance at photographs over their Sunday supplements.
**Marks:** 20
**AOs:** AO2,AO3
**Focus Poem:** War Photographer
**Focus Poet:** Carol Ann Duffy
