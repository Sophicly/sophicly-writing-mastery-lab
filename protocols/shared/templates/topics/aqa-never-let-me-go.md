# Topic 1: Kathy H
**Type:** A-MT
**Teaching Point:** Kathy H is the narrator and protagonist whose calm, retrospective voice controls the reader's access to the truth about Hailsham and the donation programme. Students should analyse how Ishiguro uses her unreliable narration, selective memory, and quiet acceptance to explore the tension between complicity and survival, and what her passivity reveals about the human capacity to normalise even the most dehumanising systems.
**Marks:** 34
**AOs:** AO1,AO2,AO3
**AO4:** 4

## Question
How does Ishiguro use the character of Kathy H to explore ideas about memory, acceptance, and complicity in *Never Let Me Go*?

Write about:
- what Kathy H says and does in the novel
- how Ishiguro presents Kathy H.

# Topic 3: Tommy
**Type:** A-MT
**Teaching Point:** Tommy is defined by his explosive anger, his creative struggles, and his deep vulnerability. Students should analyse how Ishiguro uses Tommy's arc -- from the boy who cannot produce art to the young man who creates intricate animals in a desperate bid for a deferral -- to explore the relationship between creativity, humanity, and the clones' doomed hope that proving they have souls might save them.

# Topic 4: Ruth
**Type:** A-MT
**Teaching Point:** Ruth is manipulative, image-conscious, and desperate to fit in, yet she is also deeply insecure and ultimately capable of genuine regret. Students should analyse how Ishiguro uses Ruth to explore the tension between conformity and authenticity among the clones, and how her deathbed confession -- urging Kathy and Tommy to seek a deferral -- reveals both the damage she has caused and the tragic limitations of remorse when time has already run out.

# Topic 7: Hailsham

# Topic 8: Memory and Loss
**Type:** A-MT
**Teaching Point:** Kathy's narrative is structured entirely around memory, and Ishiguro uses her selective, digressive storytelling to explore how we use memory to preserve what we have lost and to avoid confronting painful truths. # Topic 9: Conformity and Acceptance
**Type:** A-MT
**Teaching Point:** The most disturbing aspect of the novel is that the clones never rebel. Students should analyse how Ishiguro presents their passive acceptance of donation and "completion" -- through social conditioning at Hailsham, the normalising language of "carers" and "donors," and the absence of any organised resistance -- to explore how systems of oppression sustain themselves not through force alone but through the internalisation of powerlessness.

# Topic 10: Science and Ethics
**Type:** A-MT
**Teaching Point:** The donation programme is the novel's dystopian premise, yet Ishiguro deliberately keeps it at the margins, forcing the reader to confront the ethical horror gradually. Students should analyse how Ishiguro uses the euphemistic language of "donations" and "completions," Miss Emily's revelations about public indifference, and the guardians' moral compromises to explore the ethical consequences of treating human beings as resources -- and how easily a society can look away from atrocity when it benefits from the results.
**Marks:** 34
**AOs:** AO1,AO2,AO3
**AO4:** 4

## Question
How does Ishiguro use the donation programme to explore ideas about science and ethics in *Never Let Me Go*?

Write about:
- how the donation programme is presented in the novel
- how Ishiguro presents ideas about the ethics of science.
