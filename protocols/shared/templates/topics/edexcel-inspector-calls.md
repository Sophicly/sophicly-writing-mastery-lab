# Topic 4: Mr Birling
**Type:** modern-text
**Teaching Point:** Mr Birling represents the capitalist class that Priestley sought to challenge. # Topic 10: The Generational Divide
**Type:** modern-text
**Teaching Point:** Priestley deliberately contrasts the older Birlings, who refuse to change, with the younger characters, who accept responsibility. This generational divide was central to Priestley's hope that the post-war audience of 1945 would choose a more just society. Students should explore how age and attitude are linked throughout the play.
**Marks:** 40
**AOs:** AO1,AO2,AO3,AO4
**AO4:** 8
**Quote Prompt:** Sheila: "You're pretending everything's just as it was before."

## Question
How does Priestley use the differences between the older and younger characters to explore ideas about change and responsibility?

You must refer to the context of the play in your answer.
