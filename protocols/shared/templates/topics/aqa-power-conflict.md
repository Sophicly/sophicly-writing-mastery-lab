# Topic 1: Ozymandias
**Type:** poetry_named
**Teaching Point:** Ozymandias by Percy Bysshe Shelley explores the transience of power through the ruined statue of a once-mighty ruler. Students should focus on how Shelley uses the dramatic monologue frame, ironic juxtaposition, and the desert setting to critique tyranny and human arrogance. The sonnet form itself is subtly disrupted, mirroring the collapse of Ozymandias's authority.
**Marks:** 30
**AOs:** AO1,AO2,AO3
**Focus Poem:** Ozymandias
**Focus Poet:** Percy Bysshe Shelley

## Question
