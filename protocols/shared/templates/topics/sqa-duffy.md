# Topic 1: War Photographer
**Type:** poetry_named
**Teaching Point:** War Photographer by Carol Ann Duffy explores the moral tension between documenting suffering and the indifference of those who consume such images. Students should analyse how Duffy uses the religious imagery of the darkroom, the controlled three-line stanzas, and the shift from private anguish to public apathy to challenge readers' complacency about distant conflict.
**Marks:** 20
**AOs:** Understanding,Analysis,Evaluation,Expression
**Focus Poem:** War Photographer
**Focus Poet:** Carol Ann Duffy

## Question
Choose a poem by Carol Ann Duffy which explores the theme of human suffering and moral responsibility. By referring to poetic techniques, show how the poet explores this theme effectively.

# Topic 12: War Photographer (Empathy and Conflict)
**Type:** poetry_named
**Teaching Point:** This second encounter with War Photographer shifts the analytical focus from moral responsibility to the poet's exploration of the gap between experience and representation. Students should now concentrate on how Duffy uses contrast between the ordered darkroom and chaotic warzones, the haunting image of the developing photograph, and the final stanza's bitter tone to examine whether art and journalism can ever truly convey the reality of human conflict.
**Marks:** 20
**AOs:** Understanding,Analysis,Evaluation,Expression
**Focus Poem:** War Photographer
**Focus Poet:** Carol Ann Duffy

## Question
Choose a poem by Carol Ann Duffy which explores the gap between the reality of conflict and how it is represented to others. By referring to poetic techniques, show how the poet explores this theme effectively.
