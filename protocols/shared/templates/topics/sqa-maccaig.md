# Topic 1: Assisi
**Type:** poetry_named
**Teaching Point:** Assisi by Norman MacCaig exposes the hypocrisy of the Church through the contrast between the grandeur of the Basilica of St Francis and the suffering of the disabled beggar outside. Students should focus on how MacCaig uses the three-part structure, the grotesque imagery describing the dwarf, and the bitter irony of tourists admiring art that celebrates compassion while ignoring real human suffering at their feet.
**Marks:** 20
**AOs:** Understanding,Analysis,Evaluation,Expression
**Focus Poem:** Assisi
**Focus Poet:** Norman MacCaig

## Question
Choose a poem by Norman MacCaig which explores the theme of hypocrisy or injustice. By referring to poetic techniques, show how the poet develops this theme.

# Topic 3: Visiting Hour
**Type:** poetry_named
**Teaching Point:** Visiting Hour by Norman MacCaig traces the speaker's journey through a hospital to visit a dying loved one. Students should analyse how MacCaig uses the present tense to create immediacy, the clinical imagery of the hospital corridor, and the emotional restraint that finally cracks in the closing stanzas to explore grief, helplessness, and the inadequacy of words in the face of death.

# Topic 4: Aunt Julia
**Type:** poetry_named
**Teaching Point:** Aunt Julia by Norman MacCaig celebrates the poet's Gaelic-speaking great-aunt and mourns the cultural and personal connection lost through her death. Students should focus on how MacCaig uses the contrast between her vibrant, physical presence and the silence of her grave, the sensory imagery of the croft, and the poignant irony that he learned Gaelic too late to speak to her, to explore memory, language, and cultural identity.
