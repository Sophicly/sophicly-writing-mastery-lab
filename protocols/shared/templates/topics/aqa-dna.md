# Topic 1: Phil
**Type:** modern-text
**Teaching Point:** Phil is the protagonist whose quiet control and moral ambiguity drive the narrative. Students should explore how Kelly uses Phil's silence, his strategic thinking, and his eventual takeover to present ideas about the relationship between power and moral responsibility — and how the most dangerous leaders are those who appear passive.
**Marks:** 34
**AOs:** AO1,AO2,AO3
**AO4:** 4

## Question
How does Kelly use the character of Phil to explore ideas about control and moral ambiguity in *DNA*?

Write about:
- what Phil says and does
- how Kelly presents Phil.

# Topic 5: Guilt and Responsibility
**Type:** modern-text
**Teaching Point:** Guilt pervades the play yet manifests differently in each character — from Leah's anxious philosophising to Phil's cold detachment to Jan and Mark's compulsive retelling. Students should analyse how Kelly presents guilt not as a force that leads to redemption but as one that the group ultimately suppresses, exploring what this suggests about collective responsibility and the human capacity for self-deception.

# Topic 7: Morality and Moral Decay
**Type:** modern-text
**Teaching Point:** DNA traces a moral arc from a terrible accident through cover-up to deliberate attempted murder. Students should analyse how Kelly presents this escalation as disturbingly logical — each immoral decision making the next one easier — and how the play's final scene, in which the group has moved on as if nothing happened, represents the most disturbing stage of moral decay: complete indifference.

# Topic 10: Truth and Lies
**Type:** modern-text
**Teaching Point:** The cover story the group constructs becomes more real to them than the truth, and Kelly uses this to explore how lies can replace reality when an entire group commits to maintaining them. Students should analyse the layers of deception — lying to the police, to each other, and to themselves — and how the play suggests that truth becomes irrelevant when no one has the courage to speak it.
**Marks:** 34
**AOs:** AO1,AO2,AO3
**AO4:** 4
