# Macbeth — Key Quotes for Mark Scheme Examples

- "Fair is foul, and foul is fair" (Act 1, Scene 1) — Theme: Moral disorder, deception, the supernatural
- "Look like the innocent flower, but be the serpent under't" (Act 1, Scene 5) — Theme: Deception, appearance vs reality, Lady Macbeth's manipulation
- "Stars, hide your fires; / Let not light see my black and deep desires" (Act 1, Scene 4) — Theme: Ambition, concealment, guilt
- "unsex me here, / And fill me from the crown to the toe top-full / Of direst cruelty" (Act 1, Scene 5) — Theme: Gender, power, the supernatural
- "Is this a dagger which I see before me, / The handle toward my hand?" (Act 2, Scene 1) — Theme: Guilt, hallucination, psychological torment
- "Will all great Neptune's ocean wash this blood clean from my hand?" (Act 2, Scene 2) — Theme: Guilt, irreversible action, moral corruption
- "Out, damned spot! Out, I say!" (Act 5, Scene 1) — Theme: Guilt, madness, psychological collapse
- "Tomorrow, and tomorrow, and tomorrow, / Creeps in this petty pace from day to day" (Act 5, Scene 5) — Theme: Nihilism, despair, the futility of ambition
