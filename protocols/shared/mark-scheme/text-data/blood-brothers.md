# Blood Brothers — Key Quotes for Mark Scheme Examples

- "The devil's got your number" (Act 1 / Act 2) — Theme: Superstition, fate, class
- "I could have been him" (Act 2) — Theme: Class divide, nature vs nurture, envy
- "Tell me it's not true, / Say it's just a story" (Act 2) — Theme: Tragedy, disbelief, fate
- "Do we blame superstition for what came to pass? / Or could it be what we, the English, have come to know as class?" (Narrator, Act 2) — Theme: Class, fate vs social inequality, moral question
- "High upon the hill there's a woman gone mad" (Narrator, Act 1) — Theme: Consequence, maternal suffering, foreshadowing
- "Why is a blood brother more important than a real brother?" (Act 2) — Theme: Brotherhood, bonds, class separation
- "Give one to me, Mam" (Act 1) — Theme: Poverty, desperation, maternal sacrifice
