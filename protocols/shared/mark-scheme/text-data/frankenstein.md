# Frankenstein — Key Quotes for Mark Scheme Examples

- "I ought to be thy Adam, but I am rather the fallen angel" (Chapter 10) — Theme: Creation, rejection, Paradise Lost allusion
- "Beware; for I am fearless, and therefore powerful" (Chapter 20) — Theme: Isolation breeding danger, the sublime, revenge
- "I was benevolent and good; misery made me a fiend" (Chapter 10) — Theme: Nature vs nurture, social rejection, monstrosity
- "How dangerous is the acquirement of knowledge" (Chapter 4) — Theme: Forbidden knowledge, Promethean ambition, consequences of science
- "I, the miserable and the abandoned, am an abortion, to be spurned at, and kicked, and trampled on" (Chapter 24) — Theme: Rejection, self-loathing, sympathy for the creature
- "Learn from me, if not by my precepts, at least by my example, how dangerous is the acquirement of knowledge" (Chapter 4) — Theme: Cautionary tale, Romantic science, hubris
