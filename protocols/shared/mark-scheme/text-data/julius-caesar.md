# Julius Caesar — Key Quotes for Mark Scheme Examples

- "Et tu, Brute?" (Act 3, Scene 1) — Theme: Betrayal, loyalty, friendship
- "Beware the ides of March" (Act 1, Scene 2) — Theme: Fate, foreshadowing, the supernatural
- "The fault, dear Brutus, is not in our stars, / But in ourselves, that we are underlings" (Act 1, Scene 2) — Theme: Free will vs fate, ambition, manipulation
- "Friends, Romans, countrymen, lend me your ears" (Act 3, Scene 2) — Theme: Rhetoric, persuasion, political power
- "Cowards die many times before their deaths; / The valiant never taste of death but once" (Act 2, Scene 2) — Theme: Courage, fate, honour
- "Yond Cassius has a lean and hungry look; / He thinks too much: such men are dangerous" (Act 1, Scene 2) — Theme: Suspicion, power, political threat
- "Cry 'Havoc!' and let slip the dogs of war" (Act 3, Scene 1) — Theme: Revenge, chaos, violence
