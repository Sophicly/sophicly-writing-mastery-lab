# Romeo and Juliet — Key Quotes for Mark Scheme Examples

- "But soft, what light through yonder window breaks? / It is the east, and Juliet is the sun" (Act 2, Scene 2) — Theme: Love, light/dark imagery, idealisation
- "O Romeo, Romeo, wherefore art thou Romeo?" (Act 2, Scene 2) — Theme: Identity, the feud, love vs loyalty
- "What's in a name? That which we call a rose / By any other name would smell as sweet" (Act 2, Scene 2) — Theme: Identity, artificiality of the feud
- "My only love sprung from my only hate" (Act 1, Scene 5) — Theme: Love and hate, fate, dramatic irony
- "These violent delights have violent ends" (Act 2, Scene 6) — Theme: Foreshadowing, reckless passion, fate
- "A plague o' both your houses!" (Act 3, Scene 1) — Theme: The feud, senseless violence, consequence
- "For never was a story of more woe / Than this of Juliet and her Romeo" (Act 5, Scene 3) — Theme: Tragedy, reconciliation through sacrifice
