# Much Ado About Nothing — Key Quotes for Mark Scheme Examples

- "I had rather hear my dog bark at a crow than a man swear he loves me" (Act 1, Scene 1) — Theme: Gender, independence, wit as defence
- "When I said I would die a bachelor, I did not think I should live till I were married" (Act 2, Scene 3) — Theme: Self-deception, love, comedy
- "Sigh no more, ladies, sigh no more, / Men were deceivers ever" (Act 2, Scene 3) — Theme: Gender, deception, faithlessness
- "Are you good men and true?" (Act 3, Scene 3) — Theme: Truth, justice, social order (irony via the Watch)
- "I do love nothing in the world so well as you" (Act 4, Scene 1) — Theme: Love, honesty, vulnerability
- "Thou and I are too wise to woo peaceably" (Act 5, Scene 2) — Theme: Wit, equality in love, the merry war
