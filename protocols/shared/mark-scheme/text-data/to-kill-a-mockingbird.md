# To Kill a Mockingbird — Key Quotes for Mark Scheme Examples

- "You never really understand a person until you consider things from his point of view... Until you climb inside of his skin and walk around in it" (Chapter 3) — Theme: Empathy, moral education, perspective
- "Shoot all the bluejays you want, if you can hit 'em, but remember it's a sin to kill a mockingbird" (Chapter 10) — Theme: Innocence, injustice, moral symbolism
- "I wanted you to see what real courage is, instead of getting the idea that courage is a man with a gun in his hand" (Chapter 11) — Theme: True courage, moral fortitude, integrity
- "The one thing that doesn't abide by majority rule is a person's conscience" (Chapter 11) — Theme: Moral independence, justice, individual responsibility
- "People generally see what they look for, and hear what they listen for" (Chapter 20) — Theme: Prejudice, selective perception, injustice
- "Atticus, he was real nice." "Most people are, Scout, when you finally see them" (Chapter 31) — Theme: Empathy, understanding, the goodness in people
