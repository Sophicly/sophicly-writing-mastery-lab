AQA heavily favours **Stave 1** (3 out of 8 papers). **Stave 5** (the redemption) has NEVER been used. AQA prefers to give dark/negative extracts and expects students to contrast with the wider transformation across all staves.

**Scenes NEVER used:**
- Stave 5 — Scrooge's joyful transformation on Christmas morning
- Stave 3 — Fred's party (where Scrooge watches but cannot participate)
- Stave 4 — The Cratchit family mourning Tiny Tim
- Stave 4 — Old Joe's shop (people selling Scrooge's possessions)
- Stave 2 — Belle breaks off the engagement

## Student Recommendation

**Examiner favourites (practice these FIRST):**
1. **Stave 1** — used 3 times. Know the opening characterisation, charity collectors, and Marley's ghost inside out
2. **Poverty / The Poor** — asked twice, Dickens' central social message
3. **Isolation / Outsider** — asked twice, Scrooge's defining characteristic before transformation

**Extract pattern:** AQA gives dark/negative extracts and expects students to contrast with the wider transformation. Stave 5 (the happy redemption) has NEVER been used. AQA wants students to demonstrate they can discuss the WHOLE arc, not just the ending.

**Strong prediction candidates:**
- **Stave 5** extract — never used, could finally appear
- **Redemption/forgiveness** as direct theme — the novel's entire purpose, never the question focus
- **Belle** / lost love (Stave 2) — the engagement broken off, never used as extract or theme
- **Family** as the main theme — Cratchits asked about but "family" never the headline
- **Fred** — Scrooge's nephew embodies Christmas spirit, never the focus
- **Generosity/charity** — the positive flip of greed, never asked directly
