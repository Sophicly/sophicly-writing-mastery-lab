## Student Recommendation

**Examiner favourites:**
- Duality — asked twice, the novel's central concept
- Hyde's frightening nature — asked once but Hyde's appearance described in every extract
- The late chapters (9-10) — Edexcel favours the revelatory chapters

**Strong prediction candidates (never asked on Edexcel):**
- Science/experimentation — the premise of the novel, never asked
- Setting (London, fog, doors) — Gothic atmosphere, never the direct theme
- Lanyon — his death from shock is dramatic, never the character focus
- Addiction — Jekyll's loss of control, a strong modern interpretation
- Violence — Hyde's escalating brutality, never the direct theme
