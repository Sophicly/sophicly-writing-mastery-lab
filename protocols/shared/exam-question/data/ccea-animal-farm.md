# Past Paper Data — Animal Farm (CCEA)

## Past Questions by Year

CCEA format: Two options per year — (a) character-based, (b) theme-based. Closed book, no extract.

| Year | Option (a) — Character | Option (b) — Theme |
|------|------------------------|-------------------|
| 2025 | Boxer deserves to be **respected** | The pigs' behaviour is **shocking** |
| 2024 | Snowball is **heroic** | The animals **benefit** from what they learn |
| 2023 | Napoleon is **selfish** | Life on the farm is **miserable** for the animals |
| 2022 | Squealer is to **blame** for the failure of Animalism | The Revolution makes life **worse** for the animals |
| 2021 | Boxer is **valued** by the other animals | The human characters are the **cause** of the problems |
| 2019 | Snowball makes the Rebellion a **success** | Animalism was **bound to fail** |
| 2018 | Napoleon is a **strong leader** | The animals continue to **believe** in the Revolution |

## Student Recommendation

**Examiner favourites:**
- The Revolution/Animalism — asked 4 times, the dominant theme. CCEA consistently asks whether the revolution succeeds, fails, or benefits the animals
- Napoleon, Boxer, Snowball — each asked twice, the three key characters
- Life getting worse on the farm — asked 3 times with different wording

**Strong prediction candidates:**
- Old Major — the ideological founder, never asked about
- Benjamin — the cynical donkey who sees through everything, never asked
- Propaganda as a direct theme — Squealer asked about once but propaganda itself never the focus
- The Seven Commandments — central to the novel's structure, never asked
- Corruption of power — implied in many questions but never the direct theme
- Mr Jones / the humans — asked about once (2021) but could be explored much further
