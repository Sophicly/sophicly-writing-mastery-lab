Unlike Macbeth, R&J extracts are spread evenly across the play. Every act has been used. AQA favours **Act 1** (3 papers) but has covered the full arc.

**Scenes NEVER used:**
- Act 1 Scene 5 — The party / Romeo and Juliet first meet ("Did my heart love till now?")
- Act 2 Scene 4/5 — Nurse as go-between
- Act 3 Scene 2 — Juliet waits for Romeo, learns of Tybalt's death
- Act 3 Scene 5 — Romeo and Juliet's morning after / Capulet's rage about arranged marriage
- Act 4 Scene 3 — Juliet takes the potion alone
- Act 4 Scene 5 — Juliet found "dead"

## Student Recommendation

**Examiner favourites:**
1. **Violence/Conflict** — asked twice, always linked to the feud
2. **Act 1** — used in 3 papers; AQA favours early-play extracts here too
3. **Individual character studies** — Tybalt, Nurse, Friar each asked once; AQA explores the supporting cast

**Extract pattern:** Unlike Macbeth, R&J extracts span the whole play evenly. No single scene dominates.

**Strong prediction candidates:**
- **Juliet as a character** — astonishingly never the direct focus despite being co-protagonist
- **Act 1 Scene 5** (the party / "Did my heart love till now?") — the first meeting, iconic scene, never used
- **Act 3 Scene 5** (morning after / Capulet's rage) — hugely dramatic scene, never used
- **Parents/family authority** — central conflict driver, never the direct theme
- **Marriage** — drives the entire plot, never asked about directly
- **Act 4 Scene 3** (Juliet takes the potion alone) — powerful solo scene, never used
