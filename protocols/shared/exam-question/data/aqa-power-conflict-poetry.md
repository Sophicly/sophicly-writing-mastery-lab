# Past Paper Data — Power and Conflict Poetry (AQA)

## How This Paper Works

AQA names ONE poem and asks you to compare it with ONE OTHER poem of your choice from the anthology. The named poem changes each year. Students need to know ALL 15 poems well enough to compare any of them.

## 9 Poems NEVER Named (Prediction Candidates)

- **Exposure** (Wilfred Owen) — war, suffering, nature as enemy
- **Storm on the Island** (Seamus Heaney) — power of nature, fear
- **The Prelude** (Wordsworth) — power of nature, fear, awe
- **Charge of the Light Brigade** (Tennyson) — war, duty, futility
- **Kamikaze** (Beatrice Garland) — war, honour, shame, family
- **Checking Out Me History** (John Agard) — identity, power, colonialism
- **Tissue** (Imtiaz Dharker) — power, fragility, identity
- **Poppies** (Jane Weir) — war, loss, family, memory
- **The Emigree** (Carol Rumens) — identity, memory, displacement

## Student Recommendation

**Examiner favourites:**
1. **Ozymandias** — named TWICE (2018, Spec). AQA's favourite poem. Theme: power
2. **Bayonet Charge** — named TWICE (2017, 2022). Theme: war/conflict
3. **Power** as a theme — asked 3 times
4. **War/conflict** — asked 4 times

**Strategic advice:**
- Learn 4-5 poems really well as your "comparison bank" — poems you can use no matter what's named
- Best all-rounder comparison poems: **Ozymandias** (power), **London** (power + anger), **Remains** (war + guilt), **Exposure** (war + suffering), **The Prelude** (nature + power)
- Each of these connects to multiple themes and can be paired with almost any named poem
