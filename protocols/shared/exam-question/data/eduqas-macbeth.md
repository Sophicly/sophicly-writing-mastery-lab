## Extract/Scene Usage Pattern

| Act/Scene | Year | Content |
|-----------|------|---------|
| Act 1 Scene 5 | 2017 | Lady Macbeth's soliloquy |
| Act 2 Scene 2 | 2018 | After Duncan's murder |
| Act 4 Scene 1 | 2022 | Witches' cauldron and apparitions |
| Act 4 Scene 3 | 2023 | Malcolm and Macduff in England |
| Act 5 Scene 5 | 2019 | "Tomorrow and tomorrow" |

EDUQAS spreads extracts across the full play — one from each act except Act 3. Unlike AQA (which clusters in Act 1), EDUQAS tests the full arc.

## Student Recommendation

**Key EDUQAS format:** The question asks students to write about the theme "at different points in the play" — answers must be more wide-ranging than AQA. Students need 3-4 different moments, not just the extract + one other.

**Strong prediction candidates (never asked on EDUQAS):**
- Ambition — the play's most famous theme, never the direct EDUQAS question
- Violence — never asked despite being central
- Masculinity — only asked by AQA so far
- Act 1 Scene 7 (persuasion scene) — the most dramatic dialogue, never the EDUQAS extract
- Act 3 Scene 4 (Banquo's ghost) — links supernatural + guilt, not yet used
- Banquo — never the focus on any board
