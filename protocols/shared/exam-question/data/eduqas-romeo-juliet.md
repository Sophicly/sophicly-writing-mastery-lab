# Past Paper Data — Romeo and Juliet (EDUQAS)

## Past Questions by Year

| Year | Question | Extract | Theme |
|------|----------|---------|-------|
| 2017 | Write about love at different points in the play | Act 2 Scene 2 — Balcony scene: "But, soft! what light through yonder window breaks?" Romeo and Juliet declare love, plan to marry | Romantic love, Passion |
| 2018 | Write about conflict between the families | Act 3 Scene 1 — Mercutio fights Tybalt, or Act 1 Scene 1 — Opening brawl, "A plague o' both your houses!" | Conflict, Violence, The feud |
| 2019 | Write about Juliet's feelings about Romeo | Act 3 Scene 2 — "Gallop apace" soliloquy, news of Tybalt's death, "O serpent heart, hid with a flowering face!" | Love vs loyalty, Conflict of emotions |
| 2022 | Write about fate and destiny | Act 5 Scene 3 — Romeo in the tomb, or the Prologue: "A pair of star-cross'd lovers take their life" | Fate, Tragedy, Inevitability |
| 2023 | Write about the Nurse or Friar Lawrence | If Nurse: Act 1 Scene 3 — bawdy reminiscence. If Friar: Act 2 Scene 3 — "Within the infant rind of this small flower / Poison hath residence and medicine power" | Guidance, Betrayal/failure |

## Student Recommendation

**Strong prediction candidates (never asked on EDUQAS):**
- Parents/family authority — Capulet's rage (Act 3 Scene 5) is hugely dramatic, never asked
- Romeo as a character study — Juliet asked about (2019) but Romeo never the sole focus
- Tybalt — the catalyst for tragedy, never the focus on EDUQAS
- Marriage — drives the plot, never the direct theme
- Act 1 Scene 5 (the party / first meeting) — iconic scene, likely never used as EDUQAS extract
- Act 3 Scene 5 (morning after + Capulet's threat) — the play's most emotionally complex scene
