# Past Paper Data — The Cone Gatherers (SQA National 5)

## SQA Format

Students receive a printed extract (~1-2 pages) with 4-5 questions testing understanding, analysis, and a final comparison/evaluation question.

## Extract Usage by Year

| Year | Extract Focus | Key Content | Themes |
|------|--------------|-------------|--------|
| 2017 | The deer drive | Violent deer cull — Duror's savage participation, Calum's distress at animals' suffering, contrast between their reactions | Nature, Violence, Good vs Evil, Innocence |
| 2018 | Duror's hatred of Calum | Duror watching/stalking Calum in the woods, irrational loathing of Calum's deformity, mental deterioration | Prejudice, Evil, Madness, Duality |
| 2019 | The storm/tree-climbing scene | Calum and Neil caught in the storm while cone-gathering, Calum's agility and fearlessness despite physical limitations | Nature, Vulnerability, Strength in weakness |
| 2022 | Lady Runcie-Campbell's decision or beach hut scene | Class conflict — landowner's attitude vs cone gatherers' dignity. The hut scene: Calum and Neil take shelter, Lady R-C outraged | Class, Privilege, Injustice |
| 2023 | The ending or Duror's breakdown | If ending: Duror shoots Calum, then himself. Calum falls "like a sacrifice". If breakdown: Duror's psychological disintegration | Sacrifice, Good vs Evil, Tragedy |
| 2024 | Neil and Calum's relationship or Roderick's sympathy | Neil's protective care of Calum; or Roderick showing compassion against his mother's wishes | Family, Compassion, Class |

## Key Comparison Question Themes

- Good vs Evil (Calum vs Duror — obvious but effective)
- Class and privilege (Lady R-C and the cone gatherers)
- The relationship between humans and nature
- Sacrifice and innocence

## Student Recommendation

**Examiner favourites:**
- Duror vs Calum — the central opposition, features in most extracts
- Nature/the forest — the setting is inseparable from the themes
- The deer drive — used in 2017, the novel's most dramatic set-piece

**Strong prediction candidates:**
- The ending — Calum's sacrifice/death, the novel's climax. If not used in 2023, highly likely
- Roderick — the compassionate young man who represents hope, never the direct focus
- Lady Runcie-Campbell — her moral conflict (class duty vs compassion) is central
- The war backdrop — the novel is set in WW2 but the war rarely features directly
- Calum as Christ-figure — the religious symbolism, never directly asked
