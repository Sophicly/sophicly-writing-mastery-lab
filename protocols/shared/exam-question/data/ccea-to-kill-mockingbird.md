# Past Paper Data — To Kill a Mockingbird (CCEA)

## Past Questions by Year

CCEA format: Two options per year — (a) character-based, (b) theme-based. Closed book, no extract.

| Year | Option (a) | Option (b) |
|------|-----------|-----------|
| 2025 | Jem **understands** the difficulties others face | Courage is **admired** by the people of Maycomb |
| 2024 | Mayella is **powerless** | People of Maycomb show **intolerance** to Boo Radley, Tom Robinson and Dolphus Raymond |
| 2023 | Scout's attitudes to her family **change** | Telling lies is **wrong** |
| 2022 | Calpurnia is too **hard** on the children | Characters react to the court verdict in **differing** ways |
| 2021 | Atticus is **respected** by the people of Maycomb | Mrs Dubose, Boo Radley and Dolphus Raymond **cope** with suffering in differing ways |
| 2019 | Mayella **deserves** sympathy | Some people in Maycomb are **treated unfairly** |
| 2018 | People who live in Maycomb are treated **unfairly** — who is treated most unfairly? | Aunt Alexandra is **dislikeable** |

## Student Recommendation

**Examiner favourites:**
- Unfairness/intolerance — asked 3 times, the dominant theme
- Mayella — asked twice, both times as a sympathetic/complex character (not just a villain)
- Multiple character comparison — CCEA loves asking students to compare 3 characters (2021, 2024)

**Strong prediction candidates:**
- Tom Robinson as main character focus — astonishingly never asked about directly
- Boo Radley as main character focus — referenced in multi-character questions but never the sole focus
- Racism as the direct topic — unfairness/intolerance asked but never using the word "racism"
- Innocence / loss of innocence — central to the novel, never asked
- Bob Ewell — the main antagonist has never been asked about
