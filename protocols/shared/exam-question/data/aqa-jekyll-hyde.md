AQA favours the **early chapters** (1-2), using them 3-4 times. The middle chapters (3-6) are underused. The final chapter appears once.

**Chapters NEVER used:**
- Ch 3 (Dr Jekyll Was Quite at Ease) — Jekyll dismisses Utterson's concerns at dinner party
- Ch 7 (Incident at the Window) — Jekyll at the window, starts to transform, slams window shut
- Ch 8 (The Last Night) — Poole and Utterson break down the door, find Hyde dead
- Ch 9 (Dr Lanyon's Narrative) — Lanyon witnesses the transformation
- Ch 10 (latter parts) — Jekyll loses control, Hyde becomes dominant

## Student Recommendation

**Examiner favourites:**
1. **Early chapters (1-2)** — used 3-4 times. AQA loves the mystery-building opening
2. **Mystery/Secrecy** — asked twice, the novel's structural engine
3. **Violence/Fear** — asked twice, Hyde's defining characteristics
4. **Utterson** — asked once (Nov 2021) but he appears in almost every extract since he's the narrator-figure

**Extract pattern:** AQA favours Chapters 1-2 heavily. The dramatic late chapters (Lanyon's narrative, Jekyll's statement) are underused.

**Strong prediction candidates:**
- **Chapter 9 (Dr Lanyon's Narrative)** — Lanyon witnesses the transformation, never used
- **Chapter 7 (Incident at the Window)** — Jekyll starts to transform at the window, very dramatic, never used
- **Science/experimentation** — the novel's premise, never the direct theme
- **Setting (London, fog, doors)** — Gothic atmosphere central to the novel, never asked
- **Good vs Evil** — the novel's core idea, surprisingly never the direct question
- **Addiction** — Jekyll's loss of control mirrors addiction, a strong contextual angle, never asked
