AQA gives TWO questions (Either/Or) each year. Students choose one. There is NO extract — it is a whole-text question requiring memorised quotes.

|------|---------------------|-------------------|

| 2023 | How does Priestley present what life is like for **women**? | How does Priestley present the different ways **older and younger characters respond to the Inspector**? |

## Student Recommendation

**Examiner favourites (practice these FIRST):**
1. **Social class/responsibility** — asked 3 times, THE dominant AQA theme for this text
2. **Eric and Sheila** — each asked 3 times, AQA loves the younger generation's change
3. **Character change/learning** — asked 3 times, always about how characters respond to the Inspector
4. **Gender/Women** — asked twice (2020, 2023), increasingly popular topic

**AQA pattern insight:** Questions ALWAYS come in pairs (Either/Or). One typically focuses on a CHARACTER, the other on a THEME. The character question often uses "How does Priestley present [character]..." while the theme question uses "How far do you agree that..." with a statement.

**NO EXTRACT:** AQA Inspector Calls has NO extract — it is a whole-text question. Students need memorised quotes.

**Strong prediction candidates:**
- **Eva Smith** as the focus — she is discussed by every character but has never been the direct question focus
- **The ending / dramatic irony** — the phone call, the twist, the cyclical structure — never asked
- **The Inspector's final speech** — "We are members of one body" — never the direct focus
- **Gerald** — only asked once, has much more to explore (class, privilege, duplicity)
- **Mrs Birling** — only asked once (2017), a rich character for class/gender analysis
- **1912 vs 1945 setting** — the play's dramatic irony structure, never the direct question
- **Capitalism vs Socialism** — the play's political message, never explicitly asked
