# Past Paper Data — Jekyll and Hyde (OCR)

## Past Questions by Year

OCR uses a **paired-extract format** — students receive TWO extracts from the same novel.

| Year | Question | Extract A | Extract B |
|------|----------|-----------|-----------|
| 2017 | How Stevenson presents mystery | Ch 1 — The door, Enfield's story | Ch 8 — Poole and Utterson at Jekyll's door, hearing Hyde's voice |
| 2018 | How Stevenson presents Hyde | Ch 2 — Utterson meets Hyde: "pale and dwarfish" | Ch 4 — Carew murder: "ape-like fury" |
| 2019 | How Stevenson presents duality | Ch 1 — The door's two faces (street vs courtyard) | Ch 10 — Jekyll: "man is not truly one, but truly two" |
| 2022 | How Stevenson presents secrecy | Ch 2 — Utterson reads the will | Ch 6 — Lanyon's deterioration: "I wish to see or hear no more of Dr Jekyll" |
| 2023 | How Stevenson presents fear and horror | Ch 4 — The maid witnesses the Carew murder | Ch 9 — Lanyon witnesses the transformation |

The paired format works brilliantly for this novel — built on doubles, contrasts, and parallel structures.

## Student Recommendation

**Examiner favourites:**
- Mystery/secrecy — asked twice, the novel's detective-story structure
- Hyde's evil — asked twice, the visceral horror
- Late chapters (8-10) — OCR uses the revelatory chapters as one of the pair

**Strong prediction candidates (never asked on OCR):**
- Science/experimentation — the novel's premise, never asked
- Setting (London, fog) — perfect for paired extracts (day vs night, respectable street vs back door)
- Utterson — the narrator-figure, never the direct character focus
- Reputation — the social code driving the plot, never the OCR theme
