# Past Paper Data — A Christmas Carol (OCR)

## Past Questions by Year

OCR uses a unique **paired-extract format** — students receive TWO extracts from the same novel.

| Year | Question | Extract A | Extract B | Staves |
|------|----------|-----------|-----------|--------|
| 2017 | Explore how Dickens presents Scrooge's transformation | Stave 1 — "a squeezing, wrenching, grasping, scraping, clutching, covetous, old sinner!" | Stave 5 — "I am as light as a feather, I am as happy as an angel, I am as merry as a schoolboy!" | 1 + 5 |
| 2018 | Explore how Dickens uses the Ghost of Christmas Present | Stave 3 — The Ghost's first appearance: jolly giant, feast of food, green robe, holly wreath | Stave 3 — Ignorance and Want: "This boy is Ignorance. This girl is Want." | 3 + 3 |
| 2019 | Explore how Dickens presents the theme of isolation | Stave 1 — Scrooge walking home alone: fog, cold, nobody speaks to him | Stave 2 — Young Scrooge alone at school: "A solitary child, neglected by his friends" | 1 + 2 |
| 2022 | Explore how Dickens presents the Cratchit family | Stave 3 — Cratchit Christmas dinner: small goose, tiny pudding, family's joy | Stave 4 — Cratchit family mourning Tiny Tim: "My little, little child!" | 3 + 4 |
| 2023 | Explore how Dickens presents the theme of redemption | Stave 4 — Scrooge at his grave: "I am not the man I was" | Stave 5 — Buying the turkey, raising Bob's salary, "as good a man as the good old city knew" | 4 + 5 |

## Student Recommendation

**Examiner favourites:**
- Transformation/Redemption — asked twice, OCR values the arc (unlike AQA which avoids Stave 5)
- Stave 3 — used 3 times across paired extracts, the emotional centre
- Contrasting moments — OCR's paired format demands comparing two moments from the same text

**OCR advantage:** Two extracts means more textual evidence to work with. The challenge is managing both extracts plus wider context in the time allowed.

**Strong prediction candidates (never asked on OCR):**
- Greed/Selfishness — never the direct theme despite being Scrooge's defining sin
- Marley's Ghost — the catalyst for everything, never the OCR focus
- Fezziwig — contrasts with Scrooge as employer, never asked
- Belle — the emotional catalyst in Stave 2, never asked
- Fear — the supernatural horror element, never the direct theme
