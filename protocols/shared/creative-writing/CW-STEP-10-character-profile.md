#### Concept: Goals vs Needs — The Key to Character Depth

The engine of meaningful storytelling is the **gap between what a character wants and what they need**. These two forces pull in opposite directions, creating the internal tension that drives transformation.

- **Goal** = External and conscious. Something tangible the character pursues (a crown, wealth, adventure) because they believe it will deliver deeper fulfilment.
- **Need** = Internal and usually unconscious. The thing they genuinely require to become whole but cannot yet see.
- **Character Arc** = The gradual, irreversible shift from pursuing the goal to confronting the need.

_Macbeth chases power, blind to his need for honour and inner peace. Scrooge hoards wealth, unable to recognise his need for human connection._

The story's entire momentum comes from this mismatch. The **climax** is where the character is forced to choose between clinging to their goal or embracing their need. The **denouement** answers: did they change?
