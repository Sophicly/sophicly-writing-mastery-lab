- **Type:** Workbook (self-paced, no AI)

- **Hero's Journey Stage:** The Road of Trials nears its end. Now you must ensure your story MEANS something — theme — and creates the right emotional atmosphere — tone.

---

#### Concept: Theme

**Theme = Your story's deeper meaning about life/humanity.**

Theme emerges from your protagonist's transformation, NOT from explicit statement. It's what your story is REALLY about beneath the surface plot.

**Connection to Universal Human Values (Step 7):** Your theme should connect directly to the values your protagonist explores. Their transformation reveals what your story says about these values.

**Examples:**
- _Macbeth_: "Unchecked ambition corrupts absolutely" (theme emerges from his tragic arc)
- _An Inspector Calls_: "We are all responsible for each other" (theme emerges from Sheila's transformation)
- _A Christmas Carol_: "Greed isolates; generosity connects" (theme emerges from Scrooge's redemption)

---

#### Concept: Tone

**Tone = The emotional atmosphere/mood of your story.**

Tone often SHIFTS across your story, mirroring the protagonist's emotional journey.

**Tone Categories:**

| Category | Examples |
|----------|---------|
| **Positive** | Affectionate, calm, cheerful, ecstatic, nostalgic, optimistic, hopeful |
| **Neutral** | Impartial, indirect, matter-of-fact, questioning, speculative |
| **Negative** | Ambiguous, bitter, cold, despairing, fearful, foreboding, tense, ominous |
