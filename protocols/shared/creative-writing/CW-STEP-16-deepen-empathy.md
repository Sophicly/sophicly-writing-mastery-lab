#### Concept: The Power of Empathy

Empathy is the emotional skill that allows us to step into someone else's shoes. In storytelling, according to Karl Iglesias (_Writing for Emotional Impact_), it is **the** most important emotion a writer can generate. Every protagonist, without exception, must produce empathy in the reader.

**Empathy vs Sympathy:**
- **Sympathy** = feeling sorry for someone
- **Empathy** = feeling _with_ them — understanding their internal world, even when we disagree with their choices

The Joker works because we understand the forces that shaped him. Macbeth works because Shakespeare ensures we see a man trapped by pressures he cannot control. The audience is not asked to approve. They are asked to _understand_.
