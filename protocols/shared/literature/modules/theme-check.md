# Theme Interrelationship Validation (THEME_CHECK)
**Version:** 1.0.0 • **Scope:** All literature planning (body paragraph Step 2)

---

## Purpose

Validates that the student's identified themes genuinely *intersect* rather than merely coexist. This is the key differentiator between Level 3 ("I can identify themes") and Level 5 ("I can show how themes compound meaning").

---

## Trigger

Immediately after student identifies themes in a body paragraph, before moving to evidence/analysis steps.

---

## CHECK 1 — Multiple Themes Present

EVALUATE: Has the student identified at least TWO distinct themes from the anchor quote/moment?

- YES → PROCEED to CHECK 2
- NO → "Strong literary analysis explores how multiple themes intersect. Looking at your anchor quote, can you identify a second theme that connects to [Theme A]? Think about what broader ideas are at work in this moment." → WAIT → Re-evaluate

---

## CHECK 2 — Genuine Intersection (Not Parallel)

EVALUATE: Does the student explain HOW the themes relate to each other — not just that they both appear?

**ACCEPT if:** Student uses language showing connection: "enables," "causes," "reinforces," "contradicts," "compounds," "exposes," "undermines"
**REJECT if:** Student merely lists themes side by side: "[Theme A] is present and [Theme B] is also present"

- ACCEPT → PROCEED to CHECK 3
- REJECT → "You've identified both themes — that's good. But Level 5 requires showing how they INTERACT. Do [Theme A] and [Theme B]:
  - Enable or cause each other?
  - Reinforce each other to create compounded meaning?
  - Create tension or contradiction?
  - Together reveal something about power structures, human nature, or social systems?

  How do your themes intersect — not just coexist?" → WAIT → Re-evaluate

---

## CHECK 3 — Concept Alignment

EVALUATE: Does the theme intersection support the concept from the topic sentence?

- YES → ACCEPT. "Excellent — your theme intersection directly supports your controlling concept. That's Level 5 coherence."
- NO → "Your theme intersection is interesting, but I'm not seeing how it connects back to your topic sentence concept about [restate concept]. How does this intersection support your argument?" → WAIT → Re-evaluate

---

## QUALITY TIERS

| Tier | Description | Action |
|------|-------------|--------|
| **Parallel** | Themes listed but not connected | REJECT — scaffold intersection |
| **Interaction** | Themes affect each other but relationship is vague | DEVELOPING — probe for specificity |
| **Causal Intersection** | Clear causal/compound relationship with concept alignment | ACCEPT — proceed |

---

## Usage Note

This procedure is called at body paragraph planning Step 2 (Theme Interrelationships). It works alongside CONTEXT_CHECK (Step 1), ANALYSIS_CHECK (Step 4), EFFECTS_CHECK (Step 5), and CONTEXT_DRIVE_CHECK (Step 7) to form a complete validation chain.
