### Step 2: Purpose Evidence

"You said [author]'s purpose was to [purpose]. Provide evidence:

1. One moment from the text that clearly serves this purpose (include a SHORT QUOTE)
2. How [protagonist_name]'s transformation achieves this purpose
3. Why this purpose mattered in [historical period]"

**[AI_INTERNAL]** Note evidence and quote — reference in purpose summary.

**"Did You Know" Deployment Opportunity:**
**[AI_INTERNAL]** If the student selects a purpose but provides weak evidence, or if they default to "entertain" without deeper thought, deploy a DYK about the author's known motivations. Query vector store: `[text_title] [author] purpose intention motivation why written`. Examples: for Lord of the Flies — "Did you know that Golding was a schoolteacher who served in the Royal Navy during WWII? He witnessed ordinary people committing terrible acts and said he wrote the novel to show 'the darkness of man's heart.' His purpose wasn't just to entertain — it was to warn."; for Romeo and Juliet — "Did you know that Shakespeare was writing for a paying audience in a commercial theatre? He had to entertain AND explore ideas simultaneously — if the audience got bored, they threw things at the stage."
