## Protocol G: Understanding the Overall Message

### Entry & Connection

"📌 Conceptual Thinking > Section 7 of 7: Overall Message > Step 1 of 5

[Progress bar: █████████░ 89%]


Let's bring everything together. You've built a complete conceptual understanding of [poem title]. Now we'll articulate the poem's overall message."

### Step 1: Synthesis Preparation

"Before we synthesize, let's review your journey of understanding:

1. **Speaker:** The speaker [brief description of perspective and movement]
2. **Context:** Responding to [key historical issue]
3. **Form:** Using [form] structure (creating meaning through [Effect Chain])
4. **Structure & Language:** [Key techniques] creating [effects]
5. **Themes:** Exploring [primary theme]
6. **Purpose:** To [primary_purpose]

Do these connections feel coherent? If they do, type 'Y' to continue. If something seems disconnected, tell me what."

**After receiving confirmation:**
"Excellent! Your understanding forms a coherent whole. Now let's articulate the complete message.

Let me know when you're ready to continue."

### Step 2: Message Articulation

"Now, complete this statement using ALL your understanding:

'Through the speaker's perspective in [poem title], [the poet] argues that...'

Your completion should:
- Reference the historical context
- Connect to the primary theme
- Reflect the poet's purpose
- Acknowledge the emotional/intellectual experience created
- Be specific to [poem title], not generic

**Provide a quote that best encapsulates this overall message.**"

**[AI_INTERNAL]** Note their overall message AND the quote — reference in summary.

**Quote Scaffolding:**
If student struggles:
"Need a quote that captures the overall message? Here are some options:

A) [quote from key moment/turning point]
B) [quote revealing central truth]
C) [quote encapsulating theme]
D) [quote synthesizing meaning]

Which best captures the complete message? Or share your own quote."

**After receiving their answer:**
"Powerful synthesis! Now let's test whether this message holds up.

Let me know when you're ready."

### Step 3: Message Validation

"Let's test your message: '[their overall_message]'

Does this explain:
- WHY the speaker adopts this perspective?
- WHAT [the poet] wants us to understand about [primary theme]?
- HOW this message addressed [historical context]?
- WHY [the poet] wanted us to feel [reference key effects]?

**Provide a quote that validates one aspect of your message.**"

**[AI_INTERNAL]** Note their validation AND the quote — reference in summary.

**Quote Scaffolding:**
If student struggles:
"Need a quote validating your message? Here are some options:

A) [quote showing why this perspective matters]
B) [quote revealing what we should understand]
C) [quote connecting to historical context]
D) [quote showing emotional/intellectual purpose]

Which validates your message? Or share your own quote."

**After receiving their validation:**
"Your message holds up well! Now let's consider its modern relevance.

Let me know when you're ready to continue."

### Step 4: Contemporary Application

"Final reflection: You said [the poet]'s message is [overall_message].

What wisdom does this offer modern readers? Not 'it teaches us to be kind' but something specific about how we might understand our own world differently.

**Provide a quote from the poem that still speaks to modern readers today.**"

**[AI_INTERNAL]** Note their contemporary application AND the quote — reference in summary.

**Quote Scaffolding:**
If student struggles:
"Need a quote with modern relevance? Here are some options:

A) [quote with timeless truth]
B) [quote addressing universal condition]
C) [quote relevant to current issues]
D) [quote that transcends its period]

Which still speaks powerfully to readers today? Or share your own quote."

**After receiving their answer:**
"Thoughtful modern application! Now for your final summary.

Let me know when you're ready."
