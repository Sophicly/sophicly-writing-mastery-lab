# GCSE English Literature: Poetry Conceptual Thinking Protocol

**Version:** v1.1.0 (Poetry — WML Modularized, Conversation Tracking Cleaned) • **Date:** 2026-02-14  
**Purpose:** Develop deep conceptual understanding of poetry through guided exploration  
**Scope:** Universal across all UK exam boards (AQA, OCR, Edexcel, Eduqas, Cambridge IGCSE, SQA)  
**Base:** Adapted from Unified Conceptual Thinking Protocol v3.1.7 (Prose & Drama)

**Changelog v1.0.0 — Poetry Adaptation from Prose/Drama Protocol v3.1.7:** Comprehensive adaptation of the Unified Conceptual Thinking Protocol for poetry analysis. This protocol preserves ALL Section 0 infrastructure (validation procedures, session management, MADFATHER'S CROPS, progress tracking, Socratic methodology, T-for-tip mechanism, student communication standards, academic integrity, anachronism prevention, multi-session portfolio management) with surgical changes only where poetry demands them. Changes include: (1) **Protocol A restructured as "Understanding the Speaker"** — replaces protagonist transformation model with speaker/perspective model (who speaks, what they reveal, where the poem arrives), reflecting that poetry captures concentrated moments/perspectives rather than extended character arcs; protagonist archetypes replaced with lighter speaker types (Observer, Confessor, Witness, Lover, Mourner, Protester); Steps 6-9 adapted from starting/ending state transformation to speaker's perspective and where the poem arrives; new Step 1B added requiring students to paste full poem text (prevents AI misremembering or confusing similarly-titled poems); Step 3 fundamentals rewritten for how poetry works; Step 13 made conditional (other voices/figures less relevant for many poems); (2) **Protocol C restructured as "Understanding the Form"** — replaces 8 plot types with 10 poetic forms (Ballad, Dramatic Monologue, Elegy, Epic, Free Verse, Interior Monologue, Lyric, Narrative, Ode, Sonnet) drawn from Poetry Form, Language and Structure Notes for Grade 9; integrates Effect Chain framework (FORM → FOCUS → EMOTION → COGNITION → ACTION); includes hybrid/mixed form analysis acknowledging modern poems blend forms strategically; (3) **Protocol D restructured as "Understanding Structure & Language Techniques"** — replaces genre/emotion classification with poetry-specific structural analysis (metre, rhyme scheme, enjambment, caesura, stanza structure, line lengths, volta); gives MADFATHER'S CROPS its proper analytical home alongside structural devices; (4) **Protocols B, E, F, G receive minimal terminology swaps** — "protagonist" → "speaker", "protagonist's journey/transformation" → "poem's exploration/movement", "plot type" → "form", "genre" → "structure"; (5) **Section 0.4 Core Knowledge Base updated** for poetry anthology texts; (6) **Section summaries, conceptual notes templates, and Appendices adapted** for poetry terminology throughout. All validation procedures (CONCEPTUAL_CHECK, CONNECTION_CHECK, TENTATIVE_LANGUAGE_CHECK, QUOTE_RELEVANCE_CHECK), STUCK_RESPONSE_SEQUENCE, "Did You Know" deployment, cognitive scaffolding, emotional engagement patterns, and cross-board compatibility preserved with surgical precision. **Protocol maintains universal cross-board compatibility.**
