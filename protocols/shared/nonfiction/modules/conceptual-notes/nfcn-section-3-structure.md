"So far we've explored WHO writes (the writer's voice) and WHY (the context). Now we need to understand HOW the text is built — its structure and organisation.

Structure isn't just 'what order things come in' — it's a deliberate choice that creates meaning. A writer who puts the most shocking moment at the very end is making a different argument from one who puts it at the beginning. Structure controls what you know, when you know it, and how you feel about it.

How is [text_title] organised? Think about the overall shape of the text:

A) **Chronological** — events told in time order, beginning to end
B) **Thematic** — organised around ideas rather than time; each section explores a different aspect
C) **Argumentative** — claim → evidence → counterargument → conclusion
D) **Circular** — ends where it began, or returns to the opening idea with new understanding
E) **Fragmented / Episodic** — a series of scenes or moments rather than a continuous narrative
F) **Contrast-Driven** — structured around juxtaposition: before/after, us/them, expectation/reality
G) **Other / Blend** — describe in your own words

Which structural pattern(s) do you see in [text_title]? You can choose more than one if the text blends structures."
