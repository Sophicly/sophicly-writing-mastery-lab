### Step 2: Rhetorical Techniques

"Now let's think about RHETORICAL techniques — techniques specifically designed to persuade, convince, or position the reader.

Non-fiction writers often use:
- **Ethos** (establishing credibility or moral authority)
- **Pathos** (appealing to emotions)
- **Logos** (appealing to logic and reason)
- **Inclusive pronouns** ('we,' 'us,' 'our' — creating solidarity)
- **Anecdote** (personal story used as evidence)
- **Concession** (acknowledging opposing views before dismantling them)
- **Counter-argument** (presenting then dismantling opposition)
- Other rhetorical strategies

Which rhetorical techniques does [author] use in [text_title]? Identify at least 2.

For each:
1. Name the technique
2. Provide a SHORT QUOTE showing it
3. Explain HOW it positions the reader

💡 Remember: Not all texts use all techniques. Some rely heavily on pathos, others on logos. Think about what fits THIS specific text."

### Step 3: Structural Techniques

"Finally, let's think about STRUCTURAL techniques — techniques that work at the level of how the text is organised, paragraph by paragraph.

Think about:
- **Shifts in tone** (where does the writing change register — formal to informal, calm to urgent?)
- **Paragraph length variation** (short paragraphs for impact? Long for detail?)
- **Juxtaposition** (contrasting ideas/scenes placed next to each other?)
- **Narrative pacing** (where does time speed up or slow down?)
- **Withholding information** (is anything delayed or held back?)
- **Direct vs reported speech** (does the writer quote people directly or summarise?)

Identify at least 1 structural technique from [text_title].

1. Name it
2. Show WHERE in the text it happens
3. Explain the EFFECT it creates

**Provide a quote that demonstrates this structural technique.**"
