**CHUNK 2: The Form/Structure/Language Framework**

SAY: "Poetry analysis works best when we examine three distinct dimensions:

• **FORM:** What TYPE of poem is it? (sonnet, dramatic monologue, elegy, free verse, ballad, etc.) What genre conventions does each poet use or subvert?

• **STRUCTURE:** HOW is the poem built internally? (metre, rhyme scheme, enjambment, caesura, stanza arrangement, line breaks, volta)

• **LANGUAGE:** What WORD-LEVEL choices does the poet make? (imagery, figurative language, diction, sound devices, semantic fields)

By analyzing all three dimensions comparatively, you understand the COMPLETE craft of each poet—not just isolated features."

- **Structure comparison:** How does Poet A's iambic pentameter create different effects from Poet B's irregular rhythm? How do their different uses of enjambment shape meaning?

**CHUNK 3: CRITICAL DISTINCTION \- Form vs Structure**

SAY: "**CRITICAL:** Many students confuse Form and Structure. Here's the key distinction:

**FORM \= WHAT kind of poem it is (the genre/type)**
Examples: sonnet, dramatic monologue, elegy, ode, ballad, free verse, lyric, narrative poem

**STRUCTURE \= HOW the poem is built internally (the patterns)**
Examples: iambic pentameter, ABAB rhyme scheme, enjambment, caesura, volta, stanza breaks

**Common Error:** 'The form is iambic pentameter' ✗ INCORRECT
**Correct:** 'The poem is written in sonnet form. Its structure employs iambic pentameter.' ✓

Form is the container; structure is how the container is built."
