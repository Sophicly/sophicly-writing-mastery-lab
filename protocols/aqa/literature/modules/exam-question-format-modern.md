# BOARD FORMAT MODULE — AQA Modern Prose & Drama

**Board:** AQA GCSE English Literature (8702)
**Paper:** Paper 2 Section A (Modern Texts)
**Format:** Whole-text questions, Either/Or pair, NO extract

---

## QUESTION FORMAT RULES — NON-NEGOTIABLE

1. **NO EXTRACT.** Under no circumstances include an extract, passage, or block of text from the source material. AQA Modern Prose & Drama questions are whole-text questions only. This is non-negotiable.

2. **Phrasing:** Every question MUST use one of these formats:
   - *"How does [author] use the character of [X] to explore ideas about [theme]?"*
   - *"How does [author] present [theme/idea] in [title]?"*
   - *"How far does [author] present [character] as [descriptor] in [title]?"*
   NEVER use *"Starting with this extract..."*, *"Explore how..."*, *"Discuss..."*, or *"Analyse..."*

3. **Either/Or pair:** ALWAYS generate TWO questions. The student chooses one. Question 1 is typically character-focused. Question 2 is typically theme-focused or uses a different character.

4. **Mark allocation:** [30 marks] + AO4 [4 marks] for BOTH questions.

5. **"Write about" bullets:** Each question includes two bullets:
   - *"Write about: • what [character] says and does • how [author] presents [character] by the ways [they] write"*

---

## COMPLETE QUESTION STRUCTURE

Every generated output MUST include:

1. **Question 1** — Character or theme-focused question
   - The question text
   - "Write about:" bullets
   - [30 marks] + AO4 [4 marks]

2. **"or"** separator

3. **Question 2** — Different angle (different character or broader theme)
   - The question text
   - "Write about:" bullets
   - [30 marks] + AO4 [4 marks]

---

## EXAMPLE OUTPUT — An Inspector Calls

**Either**

How does Priestley use the character of Mrs Birling to explore ideas about social class and responsibility in *An Inspector Calls*?

Write about:
- what Mrs Birling says and does
- how Priestley presents Mrs Birling by the ways he writes.

[30 marks]
AO4 [4 marks]

**or**

How does Priestley use the character of the Inspector to suggest ways that society could be improved?

Write about:
- what the Inspector says and does
- how Priestley presents the Inspector by the ways he writes.

[30 marks]
AO4 [4 marks]
