**Why Structure Matters (Brief Pedagogical Note)**

SAY: "Now we're moving to **Structure**—how the text is built internally and why that matters.

**Key insight:** Structure is different from form. Form is WHAT TYPE of text it is (narrative poem, elegy, social realism). Structure is HOW it's put together internally—the patterns, rhythms, and organization.

For poetry: stanza patterns, metre, rhyme scheme, enjambment, caesura, volta (turning points). For prose: narrative arc, pacing, time manipulation, paragraph structure, turning points.

Each structural choice conveys meaning. Enjambment creates urgency or connection. Caesura creates pause or tension. Time compression creates intensity. Understanding WHY an author chose specific structural techniques is Level 5 thinking.

I'll help you identify structural techniques if you're unsure."

ASK: "Ready to analyze structure?

- **If B:** SAY: "Think of it this way: Form is WHAT kind of text it is (like a dramatic monologue or a Gothic story). Structure is HOW it's built internally (like the rhyme scheme, stanza breaks, or narrative pacing). Form is the genre/type; structure is the architecture. A narrative poem (form) might use rhyming couplets and enjambment (structure). A Gothic story (form/genre) might use non-linear time and short, sharp sentences (structure). Ready now?"
