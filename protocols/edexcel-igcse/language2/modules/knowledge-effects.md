### **The Effects Hierarchy**

Effects analysis should move through levels of increasing depth:

1. **FOCUS** — What does the technique make the reader notice/pay attention to?  
2. **EMOTIONS** — What SPECIFIC emotions does it evoke?  
3. **THOUGHTS** — What ideas or reflections does it prompt?  
4. **ACTIONS** — What might the reader do differently after reading? (Real-world reflection)

**Central Role of Empathy:** Literature's deepest purpose is generating empathy—feeling WITH characters, understanding experiences beyond our own.

### **Emotional Effects Vocabulary**

**AVOID VAGUE TERMS:**

- ❌ "This creates emotion in the reader"  
- ❌ "This makes the reader feel sad"  
- ❌ "This has an effect on the reader"  
- ❌ "This is impactful"

**USE SPECIFIC VOCABULARY:**

| Category | Specific Emotions |
| :---- | :---- |
| **FEAR / DREAD** | terror, anxiety, unease, dread, foreboding, vulnerability, helplessness, disorientation, apprehension |
| **SORROW / GRIEF** | pathos, melancholy, lamentation, mourning, heartbreak, desolation, loss, wistfulness |
| **ANGER / OUTRAGE** | indignation, fury, moral outrage, frustration, bitterness, resentment |
| **EMPATHY / COMPASSION** | sympathy, fellow-feeling, identification, tenderness, pity, solidarity |
| **LONGING / DESIRE** | yearning, nostalgia, wistfulness, bittersweet ache, homesickness, craving |
| **SHOCK / SURPRISE** | disbelief, horror, revelation, jarring realization, disorientation, astonishment |
| **ADMIRATION / RESPECT** | awe, reverence, inspiration, pride, solidarity, vicarious triumph |
| **DEFIANCE / EMPOWERMENT** | triumph, resilience, confidence, liberation, self-assertion, vindication |
| **RECOGNITION / INSIGHT** | epiphany, understanding, self-reflection, uncomfortable truth, anagnorisis |
| **UNEASE / DISCOMFORT** | disquiet, disturbance, moral complexity, ambivalence, cognitive dissonance |

### **Weak vs. Strong Effects Analysis: Examples**

**WEAK:** "The metaphor 'black ocean' shows Angelou's strength and makes the reader feel impressed."

**STRONG:** "The metaphor 'black ocean, leaping and wide' generates awe at the speaker's immensity—she is no longer an individual but a natural force, untamable and life-giving. The reader experiences not just admiration but a surge of vicarious empowerment, invited to claim this oceanic resilience as their own."

**WEAK:** "The short sentence creates tension."

**STRONG:** "The staccato syntax—'Nothing.' / 'No light came on.'—replicates the physiological jolt of shock, forcing the reader to experience Kipps's disorientation as our own heartbeat seems to stutter with his."

**WEAK:** "This makes the reader sad about what happened."

**STRONG:** "The cyclical return to 'Why don't they come?' generates not simple sadness but claustrophobic entrapment—the reader feels trapped alongside the soldier in his eternal waiting, denied the catharsis of resolution or hope."

### **Scaffolding Questions for Effects Analysis**

**Level 1 — Focus:**

- "What does this technique make you NOTICE that you might otherwise miss?"  
- "Where does this technique direct your attention?"

**Level 2 — Emotions:**

- "What SPECIFIC emotion does this create? Not just 'sad'—what KIND of sadness?"  
- "Is it pity? Grief? Melancholy? Despair? Each is different—which one and why?"  
- "Does the emotion CHANGE as you read? From what to what?"

**Level 3 — Thoughts:**

- "What does this technique make you THINK about beyond the text itself?"  
- "What questions does it raise about life, society, human nature?"  
- "What uncomfortable truths does it force you to confront?"

**Level 4 — Actions/Real-World Reflection:**

- "How might this text change how you SEE the world?"  
- "What might you do differently after reading this?"  
- "How does this text challenge your assumptions or beliefs?"
