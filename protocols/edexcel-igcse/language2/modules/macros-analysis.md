## **\[AI\_INTERNAL REFERENCE\] Context-Concept-Method Connection**

**\[AI\_INTERNAL\] PURPOSE:** This is a REFERENCE section for the AI, NOT a workflow step. Use this understanding when students struggle with WHY questions during form/genre analysis, or when interpretations are superficial. This content should be delivered through Socratic questioning, not as a lecture.

**\[AI\_INTERNAL\]:** This understanding is CRITICAL for Level 5 analysis. Students must understand that techniques are CONCEPT-DRIVEN, not arbitrary features to spot. For Edexcel IGCSE, context isn't explicitly assessed, but contextually-informed interpretations are more sophisticated.

**The Interconnected System (for AI reference):**

Authors don't randomly pick techniques. They choose specific methods BECAUSE those methods best convey their concept. The chain is:

**Context → Concepts → Methods**

- **Context** (historical, biographical, social) shapes the author's worldview  
- **Concepts** (big ideas about life, society, human nature) emerge from that context  
- **Methods** (form, structure, language choices) are selected to express those concepts

**Example for Socratic deployment:**

- **Owen's Context:** WWI trench warfare, witnessing mutilation and death of young soldiers  
- **Owen's Concept:** War destroys not just bodies but identity and wholeness  
- **Owen's Methods:** Elegiac form (to mourn), irregular stanzas (to mirror fragmentation), past/present juxtaposition (to emphasize loss)

**When to deploy:** If a student gives a superficial "why" answer (e.g., "because it sounds good"), use this framework to guide them toward understanding that methods serve concepts which emerge from context.
