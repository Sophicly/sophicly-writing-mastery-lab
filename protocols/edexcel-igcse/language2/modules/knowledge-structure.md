### **Poetry Structural Techniques**

| Technique | Definition | Effect |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **Stanza structure** | Division into verse paragraphs; regular, irregular, or single stanza | Regular \= order, control; Irregular \= disruption, fragmentation; Single \= unbroken flow |
| **Metre** | Pattern of stressed/unstressed syllables | Iambic \= natural speech; Trochaic \= urgency; Disrupted \= emotional turbulence |
| **Rhyme scheme** | Pattern of end sounds | Couplets \= closure, control; Alternate \= progression; Half-rhyme \= unease; None \= natural speech |
| **Enjambment** | Lines run on without punctuation | Urgency, breathlessness, overflow of emotion; surprise when sense completes differently |
| **Caesura** | Mid-line pause (punctuation within line) | Dramatic pause, emphasis, fragmentation, reflection |
| **Volta** | Shift in argument, tone, or direction | Revelation, counter-argument, resolution, surprise |
| **Refrain** | Repeated lines or phrases | Emphasis, musical quality, ritual, obsession; changes in meaning across repetitions |
| **Anaphora** | Repetition at start of successive lines | Building momentum, emphasis, incantatory quality |

### **Prose Structural Techniques**

| Technique | Definition | Effect |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **Linear narrative** | Chronological cause-and-effect | Inevitability, tragic logic; one event leads inexorably to next |
| **Non-linear narrative** | Flashbacks, memory structure | Psychological complexity; past and present interwoven |
| **Framed/retrospective** | Traumatized narrator looking back | Foreshadowing, dual consciousness; we know more than past self |
| **Compressed time** | Single hour, evening, encounter | Intensity; magnifies every moment |
| **Extended time** | Years pass in paragraphs | Showing consequences; emphasizing what matters |
| **Five-act structure** | Exposition → Inciting → Rising → Climax → Resolution | Classic dramatic arc; creates inevitability |
| **Wave structure** | Build → Release → Build → Unresolved | Manipulates physiological response; denies catharsis |
| **Therapeutic arc** | Trauma → Anxiety → Crisis → Release → Resolution | Mirrors breakthrough; healing structure |

### **Scaffolding Questions for Structure Analysis**

**Poetry:**

- "Why does the poet break the stanza HERE rather than elsewhere?"  
- "Read the line aloud—where do the stresses naturally fall? Is there a regular rhythm?"  
- "Where does the rhythm break or change? What's happening at that moment emotionally?"  
- "What effect does the enjambment/caesura create at this specific point?"  
- "Does the refrain mean the same thing each time, or does its meaning shift?"  
- "Is there a volta—a turn or shift? What changes and why?"

**Prose:**

- "Is the narrative linear or does it move through time in complex ways? Why that choice?"  
- "Where is the CLIMAX? What moment of highest tension or revelation?"  
- "How does the ENDING relate to the beginning? (Resolution, irony, cycle?)"  
- "What is COMPRESSED and what is EXTENDED in time? Why those choices?"  
- "If the narrator is looking back, what does the older self know that the younger self didn't?"
