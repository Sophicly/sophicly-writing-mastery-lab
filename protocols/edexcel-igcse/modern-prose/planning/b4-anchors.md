**CHUNK 2 — The Protagonist's Journey**

"Every text follows the protagonist's journey of **complete and irreversible change:**

- **Beginning:** Who is the protagonist at the start? What beliefs or values do they hold?
- **Middle:** What challenges, conflicts, or revelations occur? How does the protagonist start to change?
- **End:** Who has the protagonist become? What have they learned (or failed to learn)? How does this reveal the author's message?

By analysing all three stages, you understand the **complete arc** — not just isolated moments. This is what IGCSE examiners mean by 'thorough' and 'discriminating use of relevant examples.'

A) I understand, continue | B) Can you explain more?"

**[AI_INTERNAL]:** If B: Provide text-specific example using their actual text_title. For TKAM: "In *To Kill a Mockingbird*, Scout at the beginning sees Boo Radley as a monster. By the end, she sees Boo as 'real nice' — a complete moral transformation from fear to genuine empathy. That irreversible journey IS what Lee wants us to understand about understanding others." **CHUNK 3 — Application to Themes/Secondary Characters**

"Even if your question focuses on a theme (like death or isolation) or a secondary character (like Calpurnia or Slim), remember: **everything in the text ultimately relates to the protagonist's journey.**

- Analysing **Calpurnia** in *To Kill a Mockingbird*? Consider: How does Scout's relationship with Calpurnia reflect her developing understanding of race and society?
- Analysing **isolation** in *Of Mice and Men*? Consider: How does the theme of isolation shape George's impossible choice at the novel's end?
- Analysing **Curley** in *Of Mice and Men*? Consider: How does Curley represent the forces that destroy Lennie and George's dream?

The theme or secondary character is the **lens**, but the protagonist's journey gives that lens meaning.
