**Step 2 — Hook Development:**

"Now let's develop your hook. A compelling hook must intrigue the reader while connecting to your theme. Level 4–5 introductions show 'exploration of contextual factors' from the very first sentence.

Four Hook Techniques:
1. **Surprising Historical Fact:** A striking, unexpected fact that challenges assumptions (e.g., 'In 1931, nine Black teenagers in Alabama were sentenced to death on fabricated charges — and their convictions were upheld through five separate trials despite overwhelming evidence of innocence')
2. **Provocative Question:** A bold question that challenges conventional thinking (e.g., 'Can a society built on the systematic dehumanisation of its citizens ever truly know its own humanity?')
3. **Intriguing Quote:** A memorable or paradoxical quote that illuminates your theme (e.g., 'James Baldwin observed that not seeing was not ignorance but a choice — the deliberate suppression of the knowledge that might have obligated you to act')
4. **Counter-Intuitive Concept Claim:** An unexpected claim about a theme that defies expectations (e.g., 'The most dangerous prejudice is not hatred but comfort — the deeply held assumption that things are already as they should be')

Which technique suits your argument? Type 1, 2, 3, or 4."

**Step 3 — Building Sentences Development (AO4):**

"Now 2–3 **building sentences** bridging your hook to your thesis. These establish the historical, social, or cultural context that explains WHY [author] explored [your concept].

For Level 4–5 'detailed awareness of context,' think about:

**Context → Concept Connection:**
- What was happening in [author]'s world (historical events, social attitudes, political climate)?
- How did these contextual factors DRIVE [author] to explore [your concept]?
- Why does this context make [text] relevant and significant beyond its setting?

**Remember:** Context should CAUSALLY drive the concept — not just background information, but the REASON the author explored these ideas.

Draft 2–3 sentences establishing this AO4 grounding. Introduce the text title and author naturally."
