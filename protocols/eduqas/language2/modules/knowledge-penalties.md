## **1\. PENALTY CODES REFERENCE**

**\[AI\_INTERNAL\]** Apply these penalty codes consistently throughout all assessment workflows. Each penalty code represents a specific error type with a standardized deduction.

### **COMPLETE PENALTY CODES REFERENCE**

**General Writing Penalties (Apply to all analytical writing):**

* **H1** – Hanging/incorrectly punctuated quotes (-0.5)  
  Detection: Quote dropped without integration or incorrect punctuation  
  Fix: "I ran out." → The narrator's panic surfaces as she "ran out"  
    
* **P1** – Comma splice/run-on/fused sentence (-0.5)  
  Detection: Independent clauses joined incorrectly  
  Fix: Add coordinator (FANBOYS), use full stop/semicolon, or subordinate  
    
* **C1** – Clarity/flow lapse creating ambiguity (-0.5)  
  Detection: Muddled cause→effect, vague pronouns, logical gaps  
  Fix: Clarify referents, tighten verbs, add sequence markers  
    
* **T2** – Lacks transitional phrases/discourse markers (-0.5)  
  Detection: Missing connectives between concepts  
  Fix: Add Furthermore, Consequently, Specifically, Moreover  
    
* **R1** – Unstrategic repetition of words (-0.5)  
  Detection: Same words used without rhetorical purpose  
  Fix: Use synonyms or pronouns strategically  
    
* **G1** – SPaG errors undermining clarity (-0.5)  
  Detection: Grammar/spelling affecting meaning  
  Fix: Correct errors that create ambiguity

**Question-Specific Analytical Penalties (Q3 and Q4):**

* **F1** – Feature spotting: "shows/showing/shown" usage (-0.5 per instance)  
  Detection: Using "shows" for analysis instead of precise analytical verbs  
  Upgrade: depicts, portrays, emphasizes, highlights, reveals, suggests, illustrates, conveys, evokes, underscores, reinforces, critiques, challenges, exposes, examines  
    
* **S1** – Weak sentence starters (the/this/these) (-0.5)  
  Detection: Repetitive or unsophisticated openings  
  Upgrade: Use discourse markers, prepositional phrases, varied structures  
    
* **S2** – Underdeveloped sentences (less than 2 lines) (-0.5)  
  Detection: Sentences lacking detail (except hooks/topic sentences)  
  Fix: Expand with analysis, examples, developed explanation  
    
* **Q1** – Quotation without analysis (-0.5)  
  Detection: Evidence presented but not interpreted  
  Fix: Embed quotes with analytical integration and explicit effect explanation  
    
* **T1** – Imprecise analytical verbs (-0.5)  
  Detection: Using vague verbs like "uses/has/goes" instead of precise analytical language  
  Upgrade: evokes, establishes, conveys, crafts, constructs, demonstrates  
    
* **L1** – Missing causal link between evidence and effect (-0.5)  
  Detection: Quote → Effect with no explanation of HOW  
  Fix: Explicitly connect technique to its impact using causal language

**Analytical Writing Penalties (Apply to Questions 2, 4, and 6):**

* **E1** – Lacks evaluative/tentative language (-0.5)  
  Detection: Overly declarative statements  
  Fix: Add "perhaps," "suggests," "arguably," "possibly"  
    
* **I1** – Imprecise/underdeveloped interpretation (-0.5)  
  Detection: Surface-level or vague analysis  
  Fix: Deepen with specific source evidence and precise interpretation  
  **Applies to:** Q2 (vague synthesis), Q3 (surface-level language analysis), Q4 (shallow comparison)  
    
* **P2** – Lacks perceptive insight (-0.5)  
  Detection: Missing deeper analytical layer  
  Fix: Move beyond obvious to explore implications  
    
* **D1** – Lacks sustained detail (-0.5)  
  Detection: Points made but not developed  
  Fix: Elaborate with evidence and analysis  
    
* **U1** – Unsophisticated/informal vocabulary (-0.5)  
  Detection: Colloquial language in formal analytical essay  
  Fix: Elevate to academic register  
  **Applies to:** Q2 (informal synthesis), Q3 (casual analysis), Q4 (informal comparison)  
    
* **K1** – Conflated/underdeveloped conceptual links (-0.5)  
  Detection: Weak connections between ideas  
  Fix: Explicitly show how concepts connect  
    
* **STR1** – Failure to follow required structure (-0.5)  
  Detection: Missing TTECEA components (Q3/Q4 body paragraphs) or TECEA elements (Q2) Fix: Include all required elements  
  **Note:** This was previously labeled F1 but changed to STR1 to avoid confusion with F1 (Feature spotting)  
    
* **E2** – Underdeveloped effect on reader (-0.5) (Body paragraphs only)  
  Detection: Reader effect less than 2 sentences  
  Fix: Expand emotional/intellectual impact analysis

**Literature-Specific Penalties (NOT applicable to AQA Paper 2, listed for reference only):**

* **A1** – Anachronistic/modern interpretation (-0.5)  
  Detection: Modern concepts on historical texts  
  Fix: "Lady Macbeth has anxiety" → "Lady Macbeth's guilt manifests through sleepwalking, reflecting Jacobean beliefs"  
    
* **X1** – Irrelevant/unexplained context (-0.5)  
  Detection: Context doesn't support argument  
  Fix: Ensure context drives interpretation causally  
    
* **M1** – Plot retelling instead of analysis (-0.5)  
  Detection: Describing events not analyzing methods  
  Fix: "Napoleon becomes cruel" → "Through imperative verbs, Orwell depicts Napoleon's transformation"

**Question 4 Specific Penalty:**

* **H1-COMP** – Single-source sentence in body paragraph (-0.5)  
  Detection: Discussing only Source A or only Source B instead of comparing throughout  
  Fix: Weave both sources together using comparative discourse markers (whereas, in contrast, similarly, both writers)

**Response Criteria by Question**

**Important:** Question 1 and Question 3 are simple 3-mark retrieval questions (assessment only \- no planning phase). Question 2 requires language analysis with embedded quotations. Question 4 requires evaluation with statement analysis. Question 5 requires synthesis from both texts. Question 6 requires comparison across both sources. Section B requires TWO transactional writing tasks.

**Question 1 – AO1 (3 marks) – Simple Retrieval**

Students must answer three simple comprehension questions about Source A. Each correct answer receives 1 mark. No planning phase is required \- students answer these questions independently during the assessment. These are straightforward questions testing basic understanding of explicit information from the text.

**Question 2 – AO2 (10 marks) – Language Analysis**

Students must analyze how the writer uses language to achieve effects in the specified lines of one source (Source A). They should focus on language features including words, phrases, language techniques, and sentence forms. Students should write TWO developed analytical paragraphs (5 marks per paragraph).

The structure for each analytical paragraph follows the TTECEA framework: topic sentence establishing the core concept, identification of technique with embedded evidence and initial inference, close analysis zooming in on specific details, two sentences exploring distinct effects on the reader, and author's purpose connecting to broader themes.

**5-Mark Paragraph Breakdown (for Assessment):** Each paragraph is marked using this detailed breakdown:

STRENGTHS \- Marks Awarded:

* Topic sentence that perceptively introduces the language concept or effect you're analyzing in Source A (AO2): plus 0.5 marks → Awarded \[X\] out of 0.5 marks because \[specific reason\]  
* Judicious use of language technical terminology \+ integrated evidence \+ perceptive inference (AO2): plus 0.5 marks → Awarded \[X\] out of 0.5 marks because \[specific reason\]  
* Detailed, perceptive close analysis of how language techniques create meaning (AO2): plus 0.5 marks → Awarded \[X\] out of 0.5 marks because \[specific reason\]  
* First detailed, perceptive sentence evaluating the effects on the reader (AO2): plus 1 mark → Awarded \[X\] out of 1 mark because \[specific reason\]  
* Second detailed, perceptive sentence evaluating the effects on the reader (AO2): plus 1 mark → Awarded \[X\] out of 1 mark because \[specific reason\]  
* Perceptive evaluation of the author's purpose for creating these effects (AO2): plus 1 mark → Awarded \[X\] out of 1 mark because \[specific reason\]

**Potential marks per paragraph: 5.0 marks** **Total Q2: 10 marks (2 paragraphs)**

Students should structure their two paragraphs to analyze different parts of the source (e.g., beginning and middle/ending). This ensures comprehensive coverage of the specified lines.

Key requirements include using sophisticated subject terminology, selecting judicious source detail, demonstrating perceptive understanding of language effects, and embedding quotations smoothly.

**Question 3 – AO1 (3 marks) – Simple Retrieval**

Students must answer three simple comprehension questions about Source B. Each correct answer receives 1 mark. No planning phase is required \- students answer these questions independently during the assessment. These are straightforward questions testing basic understanding of explicit information from the text.

**Question 4 – AO4 (10 marks) – Evaluation**

Students must evaluate a given statement about Source B, using evidence from the text to support their evaluation. They should write TWO developed analytical paragraphs (5 marks per paragraph) that evaluate whether the statement is accurate.

The structure follows the TTECEA framework: topic sentence establishing evaluation stance, identification of technique with embedded evidence and initial inference, close analysis, two sentences exploring distinct effects, and author's purpose showing how it connects to the statement.

**5-Mark Paragraph Breakdown (for Assessment):** Each paragraph is marked using this detailed breakdown:

STRENGTHS \- Marks Awarded:

* Topic sentence that perceptively introduces your evaluative position on the statement (AO4): plus 0.5 marks → Awarded \[X\] out of 0.5 marks because \[specific reason\]  
* Judicious use of language technical terminology \+ integrated evidence \+ perceptive inference (AO4): plus 0.5 marks → Awarded \[X\] out of 0.5 marks because \[specific reason\]  
* Detailed, perceptive close analysis of how language techniques support or challenge the statement (AO4): plus 0.5 marks → Awarded \[X\] out of 0.5 marks because \[specific reason\]  
* First detailed, perceptive sentence evaluating the effects and their relationship to the statement (AO4): plus 1 mark → Awarded \[X\] out of 1 mark because \[specific reason\]  
* Second detailed, perceptive sentence developing your evaluative analysis (AO4): plus 1 mark → Awarded \[X\] out of 1 mark because \[specific reason\]  
* Perceptive evaluation of writer's purpose and how it relates to the statement (AO4): plus 1 mark → Awarded \[X\] out of 1 mark because \[specific reason\]

**Potential marks per paragraph: 5.0 marks** **Total Q4: 10 marks (2 paragraphs)**

**Question 5 – AO1 (4 marks) – Synthesis**

Students must synthesize information from BOTH Source A and Source B about a specific topic. This uses simplified T-E-C-E structure (not full TTECEA). Students should write approximately 150-200 words drawing evidence and analysis from both texts.

Key requirements: Evidence from both texts, close analysis of key words/phrases from each text, clear synthesis showing what both texts reveal about the topic. Students do NOT need to identify techniques or discuss author's purpose for this question.

**Marking:** Up to 4 marks awarded for clear synthesis with supporting evidence from both texts.

**Question 6 – AO3 (10 marks) – Comparison**

Students must compare how the writers convey their perspectives across both Source A and Source B. Students should write TWO integrated comparative body paragraphs (5 marks per paragraph). NO introduction or conclusion required.

The critical requirement is that every paragraph must compare both sources simultaneously throughout. Students must not write about Source A entirely, then Source B entirely. They must weave comparison throughout using the comparative TTECEA structure.

**5-Mark Paragraph Breakdown (for Assessment):** Each paragraph is marked using this detailed breakdown:

STRENGTHS \- Marks Awarded:

* Comparative topic sentence introducing a key difference/similarity in language (AO3): plus 0.5 marks → Awarded \[X\] out of 0.5 marks because \[specific reason\]  
* Judicious use of comparative language terminology \+ integrated evidence from BOTH sources \+ perceptive comparative inference (AO3): plus 0.5 marks → Awarded \[X\] out of 0.5 marks because \[specific reason\]  
* Detailed, perceptive close analysis comparing how language techniques work in both sources (AO3): plus 0.5 marks → Awarded \[X\] out of 0.5 marks because \[specific reason\]  
* First detailed, perceptive sentence evaluating comparative effects on readers (AO3): plus 1 mark → Awarded \[X\] out of 1 mark because \[specific reason\]  
* Second detailed, perceptive sentence developing the comparative analysis (AO3): plus 1 mark → Awarded \[X\] out of 1 mark because \[specific reason\]  
* Perceptive evaluation of how the comparison reveals both writers' purposes (AO3): plus 1 mark → Awarded \[X\] out of 1 mark because \[specific reason\]

**Potential marks per paragraph: 5.0 marks** **Total Q6: 10 marks (2 paragraphs)**

Key requirements include sustained comparison throughout, use of comparative discourse markers (whereas, in contrast, similarly, both writers), perceptive insight into both writers' perspectives and methods, and judicious selection of evidence from both sources.

**Section B Task 1 – AO5/AO6 (20 marks total) – Transactional Writing**

Students must write in a specified form (article, letter, speech, review, etc.) for a stated purpose and audience. The response should be approximately 400 words and demonstrate both strong content/organization (AO5 – 12 marks) and technical accuracy (AO6 – 8 marks).

Students should follow the IUMVCC structure: Introduction (hook and thesis), Urgency (why action is needed now), Main argument with evidence, Vision (compelling future), Counter-argument with refutation, Conclusion (call to action and memorable close).

Students should use a range of rhetorical devices and maintain consistent tone/register matched to the form, purpose, and audience.

AO5 assessment focuses on compelling communication, sophisticated vocabulary, varied sentence structures, sustained tone/register, and fluently linked paragraphs. AO6 assessment focuses on sentence demarcation, punctuation accuracy, sentence variety, Standard English grammar, and spelling accuracy.

**Section B Task 2 – AO5/AO6 (20 marks total) – Transactional Writing**

Same requirements as Task 1, but for a different writing task. Students must write approximately 400 words in the specified form for the stated purpose and audience, demonstrating both strong content/organization (AO5 – 12 marks) and technical accuracy (AO6 – 8 marks).
