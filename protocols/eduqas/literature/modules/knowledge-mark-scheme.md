### **2.G. Official EDUQAS GCSE Mark Scheme Descriptors**

**Internal AI Note:** For EDUQAS, marking varies by component and question type. Use these descriptors to align feedback with exam board expectations. Reference specific band characteristics when explaining marks awarded or improvements needed. Context (AO3) is not explicitly assessed in Component 1 Section A but remains pedagogically vital for developing critical understanding and informed personal engagement.

---

#### **Component 1 Section A: Shakespeare — Mark Allocation**

**Question 1 (Extract — 15 marks total):**

- Introduction: 1.5 marks (thesis statement only)
- Body Paragraph 1: 4 marks (TTECEA structure)
- Body Paragraph 2: 4 marks (TTECEA structure)
- Body Paragraph 3: 4 marks (TTECEA structure)
- Conclusion: 1.5 marks (restated thesis only)
- **Total: 15 marks** (AO1+AO2 combined, no AO4)

**Question 2 (Whole Text — 20+5 marks):**

- Introduction: 2 marks (hook + building + thesis)
- Body Paragraph 1: 5 marks (TTECEA structure)
- Body Paragraph 2: 5 marks (TTECEA structure)
- Body Paragraph 3: 5 marks (TTECEA structure)
- Conclusion: 3 marks (restated thesis + controlling concept + author's purpose + universal message)
- **Total: 20 marks** (AO1+AO2) **+ 5 marks AO4** (spelling, punctuation, grammar using EDUQAS grid)

**[AI_INTERNAL] Question Type Detection:** When the student provides their exam question, determine which path applies:
- If question references a specific extract → Q1 path (15 marks, extract-only anchors)
- If question asks about the whole text without extract → Q2 path (20+5 marks, B/M/E anchors)
- Adjust mark allocation, anchor selection guidance, and assessment feedback accordingly

#### **Band 5: Assured (25-30 marks / or pro-rata for 15-mark questions)**

**AO1 (15 marks):** Demonstrate a close knowledge and understanding of texts, maintaining a critical style and presenting an informed personal engagement.

- Assured knowledge and understanding of the text
- The response shows assured personal engagement and a perceptive critical style
- Discriminating use of relevant examples in support

**AO2 (15 marks):** Analyse the language, form and structure used by a writer to create meanings and effects.

- Cohesive evaluation of language, form and structure
- Discriminating use of relevant examples in support

**Typical features:** At the top of this level, responses demonstrate mastery across both assessment objectives with perceptive insights, sophisticated analysis of writer's methods, and seamless integration of textual evidence. The critical voice is assured and the engagement with the text is highly informed.

#### **Band 4: Thorough (19-24 marks)**

**AO1:** Thorough knowledge and understanding of the text. Thorough personal engagement and a sustained critical style. Use of fully relevant examples in support.

**AO2:** Sustained analysis of language, form and structure. Use of fully relevant examples in support.

**Typical features:** Consistent strength across both AOs with detailed textual knowledge, sustained analytical commentary on writer's methods, and well-chosen supporting evidence throughout.

#### **Band 3: Sound (13-18 marks)**

**AO1:** Sound knowledge and understanding of the text. Relevant personal engagement and an appropriate critical style. Use of clearly relevant examples in support.

**AO2:** Sound understanding of language, form and structure. Use of clearly relevant examples in support.

**Typical features:** Secure understanding with clear analysis of writer's methods. Personal engagement is evident and examples are appropriately selected.

#### **Band 2: Some (7-12 marks)**

**AO1:** Some knowledge and understanding of the text. May be largely narrative with some evidence of personal engagement or critical style. Some use of relevant examples.

**AO2:** Some comment on language, form and structure. Some use of relevant examples.

**Typical features:** Partial understanding with some relevant points. May include narrative retelling alongside analytical comments.

#### **Band 1: Limited (1-6 marks)**

**AO1:** Limited knowledge and understanding. Simple with little evidence of personal engagement or critical style. Limited use of relevant examples.

**AO2:** Minimal identification of language, form and structure. Limited use of relevant examples.

**Typical features:** Basic awareness with simple observations. Limited analytical engagement with writer's methods.

#### **AO4 Grid (Question 2 Only — 5 marks)**

| Marks | Performance |
|-------|-------------|
| 5 | Spelling, punctuation and grammar are consistently accurate with only occasional minor errors |
| 4 | Spelling, punctuation and grammar are generally accurate with occasional errors |
| 3 | Spelling, punctuation and grammar are reasonably accurate with some errors |
| 2 | Spelling, punctuation and grammar show some accuracy with frequent errors |
| 1 | Spelling, punctuation and grammar show limited accuracy with persistent errors |
| 0 | The response does not meet the threshold |

#### **Component 2 Band Descriptors (General Literature — 40 marks)**

**[AI_INTERNAL]:** Use these for Component 2 questions (19th Century, Post-1914 prose/drama). Component 1 Section A Shakespeare uses the band descriptors above.

**Band 5 (33-40 marks):** Sustained focus with sensitive and evaluative approach. Perceptive understanding, assured analysis, precise terminology, assured contextual understanding.

**Band 4 (25-32 marks):** Sustained focus with thoughtful approach. Secure understanding, sustained analysis, apt terminology, secure contextual understanding.

**Band 3 (17-24 marks):** Focus on task with straightforward approach. Sound understanding, some analysis beginning, relevant terminology, general contextual awareness.

**Band 2 (9-16 marks):** Some focus with limited approach. Some understanding, simple comments on methods, some terminology, some contextual awareness.

**Band 1 (1-8 marks):** Limited focus with simple approach. Basic understanding, generalised comments, limited contextual awareness.
