# BOARD FORMAT MODULE — EDUQAS Literature

**Board:** EDUQAS (WJEC) GCSE English Literature
**Papers:** Component 1 (Shakespeare + Poetry) + Component 2 (Post-1914 + 19th Century + Unseen)
**Format:** Extract-based (Shakespeare + 19th C) or whole-text (Post-1914), "Write about... at different points" phrasing

---

## QUESTION FORMAT RULES — NON-NEGOTIABLE

### For Shakespeare (Component 1, Section A) — EXTRACT-BASED:

1. **Phrasing:** *"You should use the extract below and your knowledge of the whole play to answer this question. Write about [theme/character] and how Shakespeare presents [it] at different points in the play."*
2. **Extract:** ~30 lines with page references, longer than AQA extracts
3. **Single question:** ONE question (not Either/Or)
4. **Mark allocation:** 40 marks (includes AO1, AO2, AO3, AO4)

### For 19th Century (Component 2, Section B) — EXTRACT-BASED:

1. **Phrasing:** *"You should use the extract below and your knowledge of the whole novel to answer this question. Write about [theme/character] and how [author] presents [it] at different points in the novel."*
2. **Extract:** ~20-30 lines
3. **Single question:** ONE question
4. **Mark allocation:** 40 marks

### For Post-1914 Prose/Drama (Component 2, Section A) — NO EXTRACT:

1. **Phrasing:** *"Write about [character] in [title] and how [author] presents [them/it] at different points in the [novel/play]."* or *"Write about [theme] and how [author] presents it at different points in the [novel/play]."*
2. **NO extract:** Whole-text question only
3. **Single question:** ONE question
4. **Mark allocation:** 40 marks

---

## KEY EDUQAS DIFFERENCE

EDUQAS expects students to write about the theme/character **"at different points"** in the text. This is a defining feature — students must range widely across the text, not just focus on the extract + one other moment. The answer should reference 3-4 different moments minimum.

---

## COMPLETE QUESTION STRUCTURE (Shakespeare/19th C)

1. **Instruction line** — *"You should use the extract below and your knowledge of the whole [play/novel]..."*
2. **Context line** (if needed)
3. **The actual extract** — ~30 lines
4. **The question** — *"Write about [theme] and how [author] presents [it] at different points in the [play/novel]."*
5. **Mark allocation** — [40 marks]

## COMPLETE QUESTION STRUCTURE (Post-1914)

1. **The question** — *"Write about [character/theme] and how [author] presents [it] at different points in the [novel/play]."*
2. **Mark allocation** — [40 marks]
3. No extract.

## EXAMPLE OUTPUT — Macbeth (Shakespeare)

You should use the extract below and your knowledge of the whole play to answer this question.

*In this extract from Act 4 Scene 1, Macbeth visits the witches.*

> **FIRST WITCH:**
> Thrice the brinded cat hath mew'd.
>
> **SECOND WITCH:**
> Thrice and once the hedge-pig whin'd.
>
> **THIRD WITCH:**
> Harpier cries 'Tis time, 'tis time.
>
> **FIRST WITCH:**
> Round about the cauldron go;
> In the poison'd entrails throw.
> Toad, that under cold stone
> Days and nights has thirty-one
> Swelter'd venom sleeping got,
> Boil thou first i' the charmed pot.
>
> **ALL:**
> Double, double toil and trouble;
> Fire burn, and cauldron bubble.
>
> **SECOND WITCH:**
> Fillet of a fenny snake,
> In the cauldron boil and bake;
> Eye of newt and toe of frog,
> Wool of bat and tongue of dog,
> Adder's fork and blind-worm's sting,
> Lizard's leg and howlet's wing,
> For a charm of powerful trouble,
> Like a hell-broth boil and bubble.
>
> **ALL:**
> Double, double toil and trouble;
> Fire burn and cauldron bubble.

Write about the witches and how Shakespeare presents their influence at different points in the play. [40 marks]
