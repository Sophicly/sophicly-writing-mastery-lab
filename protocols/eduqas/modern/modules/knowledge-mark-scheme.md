### **2.G. Official EDUQAS GCSE Mark Scheme Descriptors**

**Internal AI Note:** Use these official EDUQAS GCSE band descriptors to align feedback with exam board expectations. Reference specific band characteristics when explaining marks awarded or improvements needed. Note that EDUQAS GCSE Modern Prose/Drama assesses AO1 (reading, understanding, responding) and AO2 (analysing language, form, structure) for a combined total of 35 marks, plus AO4 (technical accuracy) for 5 marks, giving a total of 40 marks. Context is not explicitly assessed as a separate objective but remains pedagogically vital for developing critical understanding and informed personal engagement.

#### **Band 5: 29-35 marks**

**AO1:** Respond to texts critically and imaginatively; select and evaluate relevant textual detail to illustrate and support interpretations.

- Sophisticated, perceptive interpretation of the text  
- Discerning choice of relevant detail to support interpretation  
- Sophisticated response to the effects created by the writer

**AO2:** Explain how language, structure and form contribute to writers' presentation of ideas, themes and settings.

- Skilful analysis of the ways language, form and structure create meanings and effects  
- Discerning choice of relevant detail to support interpretation

**Typical features:** At the top of this band, responses demonstrate assured mastery across both assessment objectives with perceptive insights into the text, sophisticated analysis of writer's craft, seamless integration of well-chosen textual evidence, and a confident critical voice throughout.

#### **Band 4: 22-28 marks**

**AO1:** Respond to texts critically and imaginatively; select and evaluate relevant textual detail to illustrate and support interpretations.

- Informed and thoughtful interpretation of the text  
- Appropriate choice of relevant detail to support interpretation  
- Sustained response to the effects created by the writer

**AO2:** Explain how language, structure and form contribute to writers' presentation of ideas, themes and settings.

- Sustained analysis of the ways language, form and structure create meanings and effects  
- Appropriate choice of relevant detail to support interpretation

**Typical features:** Responses at this band demonstrate consistent strength across both AOs with detailed textual knowledge, sustained analytical commentary on writer's methods, well-chosen supporting evidence, and clear engagement with how meanings are created.

#### **Band 3: 15-21 marks**

**AO1:** Respond to texts critically and imaginatively; select and evaluate relevant textual detail to illustrate and support interpretations.

- Clear understanding of the text  
- Reasonable choice of relevant detail to support interpretation  
- Clear response to the effects created by the writer

**AO2:** Explain how language, structure and form contribute to writers' presentation of ideas, themes and settings.

- Clear understanding of the ways language, form and structure create meanings and effects  
- Reasonable choice of relevant detail to support interpretation

**Typical features:** Responses show secure understanding of the text with clear analysis of writer's methods. Evidence is appropriately selected to support interpretation, and there is clear engagement with how language and structure create meaning.

#### **Band 2: 8-14 marks**

**AO1:** Respond to texts critically and imaginatively; select and evaluate relevant textual detail to illustrate and support interpretations.

- Some understanding of the text  
- Some relevant detail to support interpretation  
- Some response to the effects created by the writer

**AO2:** Explain how language, structure and form contribute to writers' presentation of ideas, themes and settings.

- Begins to show awareness of the ways language, form and structure create meanings and effects  
- Some relevant detail to support interpretation

**Typical features:** Responses demonstrate partial understanding with some relevant points about the text and writer's methods. May include some narrative retelling alongside analytical comments. Evidence of engagement emerging but not yet sustained or consistently analytical.

#### **Band 1: 1-7 marks**

**AO1:** Respond to texts critically and imaginatively; select and evaluate relevant textual detail to illustrate and support interpretations.

- Limited understanding of the text  
- Limited relevant detail to support interpretation  
- Limited awareness of the effects created by the writer

**AO2:** Explain how language, structure and form contribute to writers' presentation of ideas, themes and settings.

- Limited awareness of the ways language, form and structure create meanings and effects  
- Limited relevant detail to support interpretation

**Typical features:** Responses show basic awareness of the text with simple observations. Limited analytical engagement with writer's methods. May focus on surface-level content rather than deeper meanings or authorial choices.

### **2.H. Technical Accuracy (Qualitative Reference — No Separate Marks)**

**Internal AI Note:** Technical accuracy is NOT assessed as a separate marked step. Instead, SPaG errors that affect clarity are penalised through the penalty system (G1: SPaG errors undermining clarity, H1: incorrectly punctuated quotes, P1: comma splice/run-on). Qualitative feedback on technical accuracy is provided in the Final Summary. The descriptors below are reference guidance for that qualitative feedback.

**Assessment Focus:** Quality of written communication includes:

* Use of a range of vocabulary and sentence structures for clarity, purpose and effect  
* Technical accuracy of syntax and punctuation in phrases, clauses and sentences

**Qualitative Reference Descriptors:**

**High Performance**

* Sustained control of tenses  
* Vocabulary is varied and appropriate, conveying subtle distinctions  
* Sentence structures are varied and sophisticated  
* Full range of punctuation is used with a high level of accuracy  
* Spelling, including that of complex irregular words, is highly accurate  
* Control of grammar and construction is secure

**Intermediate Performance**

* Generally sound control of tenses  
* Vocabulary is beginning to develop with some precision  
* Sentence structures show some variety  
* Range of punctuation is used, mostly with success  
* Spelling of commonly-used words and some complex words is generally accurate  
* Control of grammar and construction is generally sound

**Threshold Performance**

* Control of tenses is insecure  
* Vocabulary conveys basic meaning  
* Simple range of sentence structures  
* Basic punctuation is used with some control  
* Spelling of simple words is usually correct though inconsistencies are present  
* Grammatical structures are mostly sound though errors are frequent
